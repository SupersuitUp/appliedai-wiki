# Roles

*The pro shapes of applied AI work. Roles forming now, across every industry, as organizations try to actually deploy AI at scale. Most have more open seats than qualified people to fill them.*

---

The standard narrative is that AI is eliminating jobs. That is partly true. It is not the whole story. Every major technological transition creates a new layer of work that did not exist before. The internet did not just eliminate travel agents. It created product managers, growth marketers, SEO specialists, DevOps engineers, and a hundred other roles nobody had a name for in 1995.

AI is doing the same thing, faster. The roles forming now sit at the intersection of technical capability and organizational judgment. They require people who understand both what AI can do and how organizations actually work. That combination is rare, which is why these roles command serious compensation.

## How the section is shaped

Each role page documents:

- What the person actually does, day to day.
- Why the role is emerging now.
- How the engagement typically looks (full-time, fractional, sprint).
- Who they work with inside the org.
- The skill stack the role rewards.

These are not job descriptions copied from postings. They are doctrine pages: an honest read on the role's center of gravity, its load-bearing skills, and the most common failure modes. Useful both for the people stepping into them and for the operators hiring them.

## The roles

- **[Applied AI Consultant](/roles/applied-ai-consultant).** The client-facing builder who designs, builds, and deploys AI systems for businesses. Combines workflow decomposition, agent architecture, and hands-on implementation.
- **[Chief AI Officer](/roles/chief-ai-officer).** The embedded leader who transforms an organization into a self-improving enterprise. Leads both the technical and human sides of AI transformation at the organizational level.
- **[AI Enablement Architect](/roles/ai-enablement-architect).** The person who deploys company-wide AI upskilling infrastructure: the platform, the skill marketplace, the integrations, and the adoption mechanics that get every employee building with AI.
