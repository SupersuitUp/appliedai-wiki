# Roles

*The pro shapes of applied AI implementation work. Roles forming now, across every industry, as organizations try to actually deploy AI at scale. Most have more open seats than qualified people to fill them.*

---

The standard narrative is that AI is eliminating jobs. That is partly true. It is not the whole story. Every major technological transition creates a new layer of work that did not exist before. The internet did not just eliminate travel agents. It created product managers, growth marketers, SEO specialists, DevOps engineers, and a hundred other roles nobody had a name for in 1995.

AI is doing the same thing, faster. The roles documented here are the ones an implementer can credibly offer or step into: the client-facing builder, the cross-organizational pattern-recognizer, the upskilling architect, the workshop instructor, the workflow streamliner, the change-management partner. Each one is a real shape of professional work that an applied AI engineer or consultant can be hired into today.

## How the section is shaped

Each role page documents:

- What the person actually does, day to day.
- Why the role is emerging now.
- How the engagement typically looks (full-time, fractional, sprint).
- Who they work with inside the king's organization.
- The skill stack the role rewards.

These are not job descriptions copied from postings. They are doctrine pages: an honest read on the role's center of gravity, its load-bearing skills, and the most common failure modes.

## The roles

- **[Applied AI Consultant](/roles/applied-ai-consultant).** The client-facing builder who designs, builds, and deploys AI systems for businesses. Combines workflow decomposition, agent architecture, and hands-on implementation.
- **[AI Advisor to the CEO](/roles/ai-advisor-to-the-ceo).** The cross-organizational pattern-recognizer the CEO retains personally. Wide context across many companies, deep experience inside several, direct counsel at the moments when the wrong AI decision would cost the entire business.
- **[AI Enablement Architect](/roles/ai-enablement-architect).** The person who deploys company-wide AI upskilling infrastructure: platform, skill marketplace, integrations, adoption mechanics.
- **[Applied AI Streamliner](/roles/applied-ai-streamliner).** The implementer who builds hyper-personalized software for individual professionals. Sits with the person, decomposes their daily workflows, and builds custom apps wrapping agentic capabilities around exactly how they work.
- **[Agentic OS Trainer](/roles/agentic-os-trainer).** The person who takes a non-technical operator from zero to a working agent workspace in a single session, then coaches them through progressively deeper levels of integration.
- **[Corporate Upskiller](/roles/corporate-upskiller).** The external practitioner a company hires to install applied AI literacy across its teams. Best delivered as a pair: a business-fluent lead working alongside a deeply technical practitioner.
