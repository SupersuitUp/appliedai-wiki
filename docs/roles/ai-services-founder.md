# The AI-Services Founder

*The founder profile for an AI-native services company: domain fluency, model fluency, and operational rigor. The unglamorous third trait is the differentiator, because the product is an operation.*

Founding an [AI-native services company](/concepts/ai-native-services-company) selects for a specific combination of traits, different from the pure-software founder profile. Build with people you have already worked with; if you are solo, ask the best people you have ever worked with, and you will be surprised who says yes. Across the best AI-services founders, three attributes recur.

## Domain fluency

You are selling to skeptical buyers in often-regulated spaces, so you have to bleed credibility. The buyer needs to believe you understand their work better than they do. Direct experience in the domain is best, but learned experience is genuinely okay; how you acquired the credibility matters less than that you carry it convincingly. What you cannot do is fake it, because the buyer in a high-trust professional market can smell a tourist immediately.

## Model fluency

You need to know what frontier models can actually do today, and design the product to ride the curve as they improve. There is no substitute for great tech here, and people consistently underestimate it. The founder who knows precisely where the frontier sits builds a product that gets better for free with each model release; the founder who does not builds for last year's capability and gets passed. This is the trait the [Sam Altman test](/concepts/the-sam-altman-test) demands you exercise on the whole business.

## Operational rigor

The differentiator, and the one most founders flinch from. Variance, throughput, cycle times, SOPs: an unexciting set of words, but you are fundamentally running an operation. You have to learn that skill set and enjoy it, or at minimum respect it. [The product is an operation](/concepts/the-product-is-an-operation), and the operator who treats throughput and [output variance](/perspectives/variance-is-the-existential-problem) as first-class product metrics beats the one who treats them as someone else's problem.

## The exemplar

YC's General Legal is the model: an AI-native law firm whose founders combine real law-firm experience (Cooley, Fenwick) with technical leadership (Casetext). The thing that makes them the example is not the pedigree, it is that they think deeply about throughput and staffing, and engineered shift work into how they serve clients to cut cycle times and attract the best lawyers. The operational rigor is the edge.
