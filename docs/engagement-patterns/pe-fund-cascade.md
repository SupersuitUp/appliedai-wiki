# PE fund cascade

*The PE function holds the budget autonomy that portcos do not. One retainer at the fund level cascades into many AI engagements across the portfolio. The PE fund is the buyer, the portcos are the deployment surface.*

---

## The pattern

A private equity fund has authority over the budgets of every company in its portfolio. The fund directors choose where to deploy capital and what initiatives to mandate across portcos. An AI consultancy that contracts at the fund level gains an authorized channel into many portcos at once, where each individual portco CEO would otherwise be a separate, slower sales cycle.

The structure looks like this:

1. The consultancy contracts a retainer with the PE fund directly. Pricing is fund-shaped, often $30K+/month, sometimes much higher (see [pricing](/playbooks/pricing)).
2. The fund creates a structured request flow for its portcos. Each portco can draw on the consultancy's capacity for a defined number of "units" or engagements per year.
3. Portcos opt into specific transformations (sales team augmentation, finance automation, customer-support deflection) through that flow.
4. The consultancy runs each portco transformation as a [pilot to engagement](/playbooks/pilot-to-engagement) shape on top of the fund-level relationship.

## Why this is the sweet-spot ICP

The PE fund consolidates ten to twenty buying decisions into one relationship. The cost of acquiring the fund is roughly the cost of acquiring one portco, but the deployment surface is the entire portfolio. The fund also brings authority with it: a portco CEO who might otherwise stall a budget conversation is now executing on a fund-mandated initiative. The hesitation cycle compresses.

PE funds also have a structural appetite for margin improvement. AI workflow lift maps directly to EBITDA expansion across portcos, which is exactly the metric PE measures itself on. The pitch lands without translation work.

## Why portco-first does not work as well

Top-down through a portco CEO can stall on three reliable failure modes: portco budget is constrained, the CEO is slow to commit to a new vendor relationship, and the CEO does not have a structural mandate to invest in transformation work across the rest of the portfolio. Going top-down through the PE fund bypasses all three. The PE function has autonomy on spend, has a relationship-velocity advantage with the consultancy, and can mandate participation across portcos.

This does not mean portco-first is wrong. It works when the portco CEO is unusually entrepreneurial, the portco is under-managed by the fund, or the consultancy already has trust at the portco. It works less reliably than the fund-first move when the goal is scale.

## What to know before pitching a PE fund

- The fund is buying margin lift, not technology. Frame everything in EBITDA terms.
- The fund expects multi-portco coverage to be part of the offer, not an upsell.
- Reference rate for fund-level retainers: $30K+/month is a real anchor (see [pricing](/playbooks/pricing)).
- The consultancy should be ready to staff multiple parallel portco transformations on day one.
