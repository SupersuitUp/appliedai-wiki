# What is Applied AI

*Putting AI to its highest and best use: running an organization as autonomously as possible so it can deliver value for humanity while maximizing the scale of the value.*

## What it is not

Applied AI is not a chatbot. It is not a robot. It is not automation as an endpoint. It is not doing more of what you are already doing, faster, regardless of whether the work was worth doing in the first place.

Each of those framings is real. None of them is the thing.

## What it is

Applied AI is putting AI to its **highest and best use** in service of the work an organization is actually called to.

The definition that travels:

> **Applied AI is the deliberate, human-led deployment of AI so that the organization can deliver value at a scale that was previously impossible, while keeping the irreplaceably human elements of the work intact.**

This is general on purpose. The highest and best use of AI for a business is one thing. The highest and best use for an artist, a city, a school, a research lab, a ministry is another. The instrument is the same. The application is specific to the wielder.

The operative word is *applied*: not studied, not speculated about, not treated as a vendor subscription. **Pointed at a goal and deployed.**

## The two schools

Two schools of thought have emerged around AI in organizations. They are not two points on the same spectrum. They are two fundamentally different philosophies with two fundamentally different outcomes.

**Reductive cost-cutting.** AI as a mechanism to replace headcount, automate tasks, and reduce expenses, with the savings as the end goal. The operative question: *how many people can we replace?*

**Generative value-creation.** AI to multiply human capacity, unlock new capabilities, and deliver impact at a scale that was previously impossible, with the mission as the end goal. The operative question: *what can our people become?*

Any well-run operation using AI eliminates unnecessary redundancies. That is operational hygiene. The distinction between the two schools is what drives the strategy. Reductive treats headcount reduction as the destination. Generative treats it as a byproduct of building something better.

The reductive approach has already hit its floor. Klarna replaced 700 employees with AI, watched quality collapse, and had to reverse course. The reductive school treats people as line items. Applied AI treats people as irreplaceable assets to be multiplied. **The organizations that define the next decade are not the ones that cut the most. They are the ones that create the most value with the humans they have.**

## The shape of it in practice

At the highest level, any operation running on applied AI looks like this: the human at the center sets direction, AI systems handle the operations that can be handed off, and the humans in the system do the work that only humans can do.

- Four hours of participant feedback synthesis collapses to four minutes.
- Every document starts at seventy percent instead of zero.
- Skills gaps get mapped in real time rather than once a year.
- Strategic blockers get named, interviewed, and turned into artifacts in fifteen minutes instead of fifteen hours.

In each case, the human still makes the call. The human is just making better calls, faster, with more information.

This is not the ceiling. This is the floor. The organizations furthest along are building self-improving operational systems: AI that monitors its own output, identifies what is working, and adjusts without being told to. The founder's job shifts from running the machine to upgrading the machine.

## Two discipline principles

**Think in eighty-twenty.** Applied AI executes against whatever you point it at. The practitioner's discipline is asking, on every problem: what are the twenty percent of moves that unlock eighty percent of the leverage here? Most of what any operator could do with AI is not worth doing. The game is picking the few moves that are. "Highest and best use" is not a slogan. It is a filter applied every day.

**Do not scale what is not working.** AI accelerates volume. It does not fix a broken strategy. If a program is not landing, if a pitch is not converting, if a relationship is not deepening, applied AI makes the organization do more of what is not working, faster. The framework comes first. The tools serve the framework. Pick the right twenty percent. Refuse to scale the rest.

## The human in charge

Applied AI puts the human in charge. It is not something that happens to an organization. It is something a human or an organization *does*, deliberately, with intention, in service of a mission that existed before the tools arrived.

This is load-bearing. The version of AI that does not keep the human in charge is the extractive one optimized by attention-economy incumbents. The version that keeps the human in charge is the version this wiki is for. The tool is the same. The intention determines which version is running.

---

> **Supercharge your people. Multiply your mission. That is applied AI. The instrument is the same in every hand; what determines the outcome is the wielder.**
