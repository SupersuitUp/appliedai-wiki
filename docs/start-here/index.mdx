This wiki is a snapshot of what the operators behind it currently believe about applying AI to a king's empire. It is **not** a finished manual. It is the starting point you reach for before an engagement so the first conversation skips the preamble.

The actual craft of multiplying a king gets unearthed in the engagement, not in the wiki. Expect:

- Substantial pruning as initial engagements reveal what was theoretical and what is load-bearing
- Substantial rewriting as the language sharpens against real principals and real P&Ls

:::

## What this wiki is for

The reader is an implementer: an applied AI engineer, strategist, or trusted operator hired or partnered into a king's enterprise to deliver AI-multiplication work. The reader has been brought in because the king's empire is too big to be replicated by adding humans, and the next chapter of the legacy depends on getting AI right.

A **king** here is the principal of an entrepreneurial ecosystem. A multi-vertical real estate operator, a founder running a multi-billion-dollar holding company, an institutional builder whose legacy is on the line. Kings are not titles. They are levels of consequence.

This wiki exists so the implementer arrives in the engagement with a working vocabulary, a working library of moves, and a working filter for what is high-leverage versus what is theater.

## How the wiki is shaped

- **[What is Applied AI](/start-here/what-is-applied-ai)**: the working definition the rest of the wiki operates from.
- **[Disciplines](/disciplines)**: the craft pillars: prompting, context engineering, evals, agents, retrieval, observability. One page per pillar as the stable home for accumulated picks.
- **[Playbooks](/playbooks)**: opinionated, step-by-step procedures for the high-leverage moves.
- **[Tools](/reference/tools)**: one page per tool. What it is, what it costs, when to use it, when not. Honest.
- **[Executive Summary](/start-here/executive-summary)**: the field in one page, the load-bearing claims a practitioner should know cold.
- **[Engagement Patterns](/engagement-patterns)**: anonymized lessons from real implementation work. What worked, what did not, why.
- **[Roles](/roles)**: the named roles inside a king's AI org. What each one owns and what good looks like.
- **[Onboarding](/onboarding)**: for implementers entering an engagement. What good looks like. What we ship. What we do not.
- **[Concepts](/concepts)**: coined terms specific to implementation craft. Flat A-Z, italic definition under each H1.
- **[Reference](/reference)**: glossary, voice rules, contribution rubric, the quality rubric we hold this wiki to.

## How to use it

Land on the section that matches the question. If you are mid-engagement and stuck on a specific craft problem, start in [Disciplines](/disciplines). If you are about to scope a new engagement, start in [Playbooks](/playbooks) and [Engagement Patterns](/engagement-patterns). If you are new to this body of work, read [What is Applied AI](/start-here/what-is-applied-ai) first, then walk the sidebar top to bottom.

The wiki does not promote, brand, or pitch. There is no contact form. The work speaks. If you have the URL, you have the access.

## North star

The operators behind this wiki want to be synonymous with kings seeing their legacies secured through applied AI. Everything inside this wiki points at that. If a page does not change what the implementer does on Monday in service of a king, the page should not be here.
