# appliedai.wiki

*Ground truth for practitioners applying AI to businesses at the highest leverage possible.*

---

This wiki is the operating manual for a specific kind of practitioner: the applied AI engineer or strategist whose job is to make a business measurably better by deploying AI to its highest and best use.

The audience is small and specific. The reader is someone who has been hired or partnered into a business to deliver AI implementation work, who has the trust of leadership, and who is being held to outcomes, not activity. The reader is not an AI hobbyist, not a tooling collector, not a "what should I prompt today" learner. The reader has a P&L to move.

Every page here earns its slot by being among the twenty percent of moves that produce eighty percent of business outcomes. This is not an encyclopedia. It is a filter. Tools, playbooks, frameworks, and concepts that do not change what a practitioner does on Monday do not belong.

## How the wiki is shaped

- **[What is Applied AI](/start-here/what-is-applied-ai)**: the working definition the rest of the wiki operates from.
- **[Disciplines](/disciplines)**: the craft pillars: prompting, context engineering, evals, agents, retrieval, observability. One page per pillar as the stable home for accumulated picks.
- **[Playbooks](/playbooks)**: opinionated, step-by-step procedures for the high-leverage moves.
- **[Tools](/reference/tools)**: one page per tool. What it is, what it costs, when to use it, when not. Honest.
- **[Foundations](/foundations)**: the canonical reads, talks, and papers a practitioner should know cold.
- **[Engagement Patterns](/engagement-patterns)**: anonymized lessons from real implementation work. What worked, what did not, why.
- **[People to Follow](/people-to-follow)**: practitioners worth tracking. Why each one, and what they are worth reading for.
- **[Onboarding](/onboarding)**: for implementers entering an engagement. What "good" looks like. What we ship. What we do not.
- **[Concepts](/concepts)**: coined terms specific to implementation craft. Flat A-Z, italic definition under each H1.
- **[Reference](/reference)**: glossary, voice rules, contribution rubric.

## How to use it

Land on the section that matches the question. If you are mid-engagement and stuck on a specific craft problem, start in [Disciplines](/disciplines). If you are about to scope a new engagement, start in [Playbooks](/playbooks) and [Engagement Patterns](/engagement-patterns). If you are new to this body of work, read [What is Applied AI](/start-here/what-is-applied-ai) first, then walk the sidebar top to bottom.

The wiki does not promote, brand, or pitch. There is no about page. There is no contact form. The work speaks. If you have the URL, you have the access.
