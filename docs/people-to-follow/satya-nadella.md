# Satya Nadella

*Chairman and CEO of Microsoft. Public-teaching source on what becomes valuable in the age of token abundance: cognitive coverage, token intelligence, and the human skills that compound alongside agents.*

---

## Background

Nadella is chairman and CEO of Microsoft, which he has led since 2014. Under his tenure the company reorganized around cloud and, more recently, around AI as a first-class layer of every product, including the GitHub Copilot and Microsoft Copilot agent surfaces that put coding and knowledge-work agents in front of tens of millions of users.

He is on this wiki for a narrower reason than his title. In public conversation he is unusually concrete about the *human* skills that gain value as agents do more of the work, and he names them in terms an implementer can build into a workflow rather than in abstract executive language.

## Why he is on this wiki

The applied-AI implementer's hardest problem is not getting an agent to produce output. It is keeping the human able to stand behind output the agent produced. Nadella names the discipline for that directly: cognitive coverage, the practice of verifying that a person has actually understood what an agent did, modeled on test coverage. He pairs it with token intelligence, the discipline of spending tokens efficiently against a measurable outcome.

Both are craft an implementer can wire into an agent loop, not just talking points. That is why his framing earns a page here.

## Contributions

- **[Cognitive Coverage](/concepts/cognitive-coverage)**: the concept page distilling his framing that as expertise becomes abundant, the human's coverage of that expertise is what becomes scarce and valuable. Modeled on test coverage; mechanized as a quiz the agent generates on its own output.

## Field Notes

A dated log of what was lifted from each Nadella piece this wiki has processed. Most recent first.

### 2026-06-05 — "Satya Nadella on making human and token capital compound" (YouTube, Reid Hoffman)

A long-form conversation with Reid Hoffman on what compounds when both human capital and token capital are in play. The lifted idea is cognitive coverage. Nadella relays a GitHub Copilot skill one of his colleagues built: whenever an agent does work, the skill generates a quiz so the human learns what the agent did, the same way test coverage tracks how much of the code the tests exercise. He frames the human's ability to deductively reconstruct what an agent did as one of the more important skills to develop, and pairs cognitive coverage with token intelligence (spending tokens efficiently against a rubric or eval) as the blended skill of the modern knowledge worker.

> "Just like how we have test coverage, we now have this new concept called cognitive coverage where we we as humans are going to learn from what it did."
>
> — Satya Nadella, [Satya Nadella on making human and token capital compound](https://youtu.be/BKx0Dp8y-6g), YouTube, 2026-06-05

> "The expertise is always going to be there in abundance. It's really your cognitive coverage of that expertise that's more at a premium."
>
> — Satya Nadella, [Satya Nadella on making human and token capital compound](https://youtu.be/BKx0Dp8y-6g), YouTube, 2026-06-05

Where it landed on this wiki: [Cognitive Coverage](/concepts/cognitive-coverage) as a concept page; cross-linked from [The Token Economy](/perspectives/the-token-economy) as the abundance backdrop.
