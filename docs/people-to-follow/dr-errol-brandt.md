# Dr. Errol Brandt

*Founder of Kiraa.AI in Gold Coast, Australia. Thirty years building analytics and AI inside global enterprises. Principal-facing source on AI unit economics and the strategic implications for boards.*

---

## Background

Brandt founded Kiraa.AI, an advisory practice based on the Gold Coast in Australia, after three decades building analytics and AI capabilities inside global enterprises. He holds a Doctor of Business Administration and is an IBM Champion, a CEO Magazine Top Tech Innovator, an FCPA, and a GAICD. His positioning is "from information to insights with AI", focused on how leaders make decisions when the substrate underneath them is changing weekly.

His public-teaching output runs on his YouTube channel (Kiraa) in short, board-room-pitched videos: each one takes a structural development in AI economics and walks through the operating consequences. The videos are dense, the run time is short, and the audience is principals who need to brief a board.

## Why he is on this wiki

Brandt is on this wiki because the implementer's work runs on assumptions about AI cost and AI supply that the principal is no longer willing to take on faith. The serious applied AI engagement now has to answer: "what does our token spend look like in three years, on what evidence." Brandt is one of the few public-teaching sources making those conversations legible to a board without dumbing them down.

His framing is also useful for implementers who need to translate their own infrastructure choices into language a principal can sponsor. The argument that local inference is a hedge against subsidy reset is exactly the kind of board-ready frame an applied AI consultant needs ready.

## Field Notes

### 2026-05-23 — "Prepare for the AI Token Rug Pull" (YouTube, 5:27)

A short structural argument with three load-bearing moves:

1. **An index now exists.** Harsha Maddipatla, Partner in Strategy at Monitor Deloitte in Melbourne, built the [AI Token Price Index](https://tokenpriceindex.com/). It aggregates input, output, cached, and reasoning token pricing across commercial models into a single benchmark, analogous to Brent crude for oil. Until now the AI economy had no objective marketwide reference price.
2. **Token prices are subsidized below cost.** OpenAI lost roughly $5 billion last year, Anthropic is burning at a similar rate. Every token bought today is propped up by the largest capital subsidy in the history of enterprise software. The classic platform-economy play: subsidize to capture, capture to monetize, monetize until the customer cannot afford to leave.
3. **The direction is unambiguously up, driven by geopolitics.** Pressure on the index is coming from energy costs and constrained data center capacity in Europe and the Middle East, not from the AI labs themselves. Companies routing every query through a single US or European region have no architectural defense against a sudden doubling of their bill.

His prescription: the businesses that survive the reset are the ones who got off the cloud before the music stopped, running local AI on hardware they own.

> "Every token you're consuming today is being sold to you below cost, propped up by investors who are hoping to clean up when they're the last man standing. This is not a sustainable business model. It's a customer acquisition strategy. And when the music stops, when the funding rounds dry up, or when one player goes bust, it's all going to come home to roost."
>
> Dr. Errol Brandt, ["Prepare for the AI Token Rug Pull"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DZAcEa5dmVQ), YouTube, 2026-05-23
