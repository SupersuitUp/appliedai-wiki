# Daniel Priestley

*Founder of Dent Global and ScoreApp. Author of Key Person of Influence and Oversubscribed. Public-teaching source on how entrepreneurs reprice their work in the post-AI economy.*

---

## Background

Priestley founded Dent Global, an accelerator for entrepreneur-led businesses scaling past the founder, and ScoreApp, a quiz-marketing platform that grew to tens of thousands of paying customers. He is the author of *Key Person of Influence*, *Oversubscribed*, *Entrepreneur Revolution*, and *24 Assets*. His YouTube channel publishes principal-facing content on offer design, brand asset architecture, and the operating economics of small entrepreneur-led firms.

## Why he is on this wiki

Priestley is not an applied-AI implementer. He is on this wiki because his framework names the repricing pressure that hits the Applied AI consultant's clients first and the consultant second. Implementers who internalize his frame build outcome-priced engagements that survive the substitution frontier. Implementers who do not absorb it keep billing hours and compete on day-rate with a substitute that does not sleep.

His content is the principal-side mirror of what the disciplines and playbooks on this wiki teach. Reading him keeps the implementer honest about what their clients are actually buying.

## Field Notes

### 2026-05-23: "Why You Must Stop Selling Your Time for Money" (YouTube)

The video that seeded the contribution above. Three structural moves:

1. **Token economics.** A full knowledge-worker year reproduces in roughly $200,000 of tokens, and the token cost is falling. Time-for-money invoicing is the part of the work that compresses to tokens.
2. **Two hard problems.** Every business has to solve speed-to-value (deliver the outcome faster than alternatives) and speed-to-market (be discovered faster than alternatives). Everything else is noise.
3. **Five things you get paid for.** Conviction (what you want to see happen), judgment (choosing path and tools), taste (resonance with the buyer), commercial outcomes (sales + delivery), improving the system (compounding the stack). AI does none of these natively.

The synthesis is the "digital soil" metaphor. In the agricultural age the farmer did steps 1, 2, 9, 10 (decide when to plant, plant, decide when to harvest, sell), and the land automated steps 3 through 8. The post-AI economy runs the same shape on digital soil; the operator does the bookends, the agent fills the middle.

> "Conviction is why do we do this. Judgment is what are we going to do. Taste is who do we do this for and how do we connect with them. Commercial success is about when do we land this plane. And systems is how do we do it better."
>
> Daniel Priestley, ["Why You Must Stop Selling Your Time for Money"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkPatBreE9w), YouTube, 2026-05-23
