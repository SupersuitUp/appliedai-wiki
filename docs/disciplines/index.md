# Disciplines

*The craft pillars of applied AI implementation. Each discipline is a long-lived home for the highest-leverage picks: frameworks, tools, patterns, and the two or three reads that compress months of trial-and-error into hours.*

A discipline page is not a tutorial. It is a curated index for a practitioner who is already in the work and needs to know what to reach for. Each discipline page answers four questions: what the discipline is, what the high-leverage moves are, what the bottom-of-the-barrel mistakes look like, and which tools and playbooks live downstream.

## The pillars

- **[Prompting](/disciplines/prompting)**: the systematic side: frameworks (CRIT and beyond), prompt libraries, in-context examples, prompt versioning.
- **Context Engineering**: context construction, RAG, memory, the discipline of choosing what the model sees.
- **Evals**: building golden sets, scoring, regression detection, the discipline of measuring quality.
- **Agents**: harnesses, planning, tool use, the discipline of giving the model agency without losing the plot.
- **Retrieval**: embeddings, vector stores, retrieval evals, when retrieval helps and when it does not.
- **Observability**: logging, tracing, cost monitoring, the discipline of knowing what your AI is actually doing in production.

## How to read a discipline page

Start with the high-leverage moves. If you are not doing those, the rest will not save you. Then check the bottom-of-the-barrel mistakes: they are the asymmetric losers that wipe out months of work. Tools and playbooks come last, downstream of the framework.
