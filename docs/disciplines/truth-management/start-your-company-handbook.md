# Start Your Company Handbook

*A systematic process for building a comprehensive company "bible" that captures and documents successful practices as your business grows, transforming tribal knowledge (unwritten know-how stored in people's heads) into scalable institutional intelligence.*

---

In the age of AI, there is no excuse for not institutionalizing your wins: the specific practices that got you to the point where you can scale by hiring new people.

## Personal and institutional wikis

Both individuals and institutions can have wikis. Both can have private wikis and wikis scoped to specific audiences (team-only, advisor-only, public). A "company handbook" is one specific, high-gravitas variant: the institutional wiki that every human and AI agent in the company operates from.

The practical relationship between personal and institutional:

- **Personal wiki** is where a leader plays with ideas, develops vocabulary, and tests concepts informally with colleagues before committing the institution to them.
- **Institutional wiki** (the company handbook) is the contract. Every entry is a commitment the institution stands behind.

Ideas graduate from personal wiki to institutional wiki once they have absorbed enough feedback and proven their usefulness. Ideas can also be pulled back down (removed or archived) when the institution's understanding shifts. Git history preserves the reasoning for every promotion and demotion.

This graduation pattern gives a leader a lower-stakes playground to think out loud in, while protecting the seriousness of the institutional bible.

## The core problem

You have built a successful business through specific ways of doing things. Now you want to hire people, but the last thing you want is for average service quality to decline because new hires are operating on assumptions rather than your proven methods.

The gap between your success and their performance is not talent. It is the undocumented knowledge that lives only in your head.

## Why this process matters now

### The AI advantage

Before large language models, documenting granular operational details was hard to justify (too time-consuming relative to doing client work). Now, with AI assistance, you can:

- Interview employees with tribal knowledge efficiently.
- Process conversations into structured documentation rapidly.
- Create training materials that work for both humans and AI agents.
- Build searchable knowledge bases from successful project examples.

### The scaling imperative

Every successful project contains lessons that should become institutional DNA. Without systematic capture:

- New hires guess at your standards.
- Quality becomes inconsistent across team members.
- You repeat the same explanations endlessly.
- Competitive advantages remain trapped in individual heads.

## The company handbook framework

Think of your truth management repository as a growing company handbook: a living collection that expands as your institution learns and grows. The core purpose is communicating "the way" your institution operates.

This means documenting detailed stories of how to do certain things and what not to do:

- How you actually care about customers (not platitudes).
- Exemplary projects broken down step-by-step and what made them successful.
- Day-to-day procedures and relationship management approaches.
- Tricky situations navigated well with principles applied.
- What does not work and why.
- How to recover from mistakes.
- When standard procedures should be adapted.

## Implementation process

### Phase 1: Capture existing wins

- Identify your most successful projects and outcomes.
- Record detailed conversations with people who delivered those wins.
- Identify and extract common themes and practices using AI.
- Create first version of key processes.

### Phase 2: Systematic documentation

- Do post-mortems after every significant success to document what made it work.
- Document approaches to edge cases (when someone handles a tricky situation well).
- Create onboarding resources from your documentation.
- Let new hires identify gaps in documentation.

### Phase 3: Continuous evolution

- Update regularly as practices improve.
- Track changes and reasoning behind updates via version control.
- Use documentation to ensure knowledge is not siloed.
- Create system prompts for your AI agents based on documented practices.

## Document both sides

A useful handbook captures both positive examples and cautionary tales. Documenting only successes hides the lessons that prevented bigger mistakes.

Your company handbook should follow the same pattern: document your successes, your failures, and your near-misses with equal rigor.

## Success metrics

- **Consistency.** New hires deliver similar quality to experienced team members.
- **Efficiency.** Less time spent on repetitive explanations and corrections.
- **Scalability.** Quality maintained or improved as team grows.
- **Knowledge retention.** Critical practices survive personnel changes.
- **AI effectiveness.** AI agents can operate according to company standards.

## The North Star

An institution where every new hire can deliver your standard of excellence because they are operating from the same documented understanding of what excellence means. Not guessing based on their previous experience or assumptions.
