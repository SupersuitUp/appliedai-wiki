# Foundations

*Canonical reads, talks, and papers a practitioner of applied AI should know cold. Not "books I read this year." The two-dozen sources that compress months of trial-and-error.*

---

The Foundations section is curated, not comprehensive. The bar for inclusion is high: a source earns its slot here only if it changes how a working practitioner approaches problems, not because it is famous or recently published.

Each entry includes what the source is, why it matters for applied AI implementation, and the one or two ideas worth carrying out.

## How the section is shaped

- **Books**: long-form reads that compound. Listed with a one-line take.
- **Talks**: single-sitting watches that compress an idea. Listed with link, runtime, and the load-bearing claim.
- **Papers**: research that practitioners reference in their own work.
