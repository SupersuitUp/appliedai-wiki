# Pilot to engagement

*The MBB firms built their long contracts on a three-month proof of concept followed by a year-long roll-out. The structure is now open-source. Any boutique AI consultancy that can execute it can capture MBB-shaped revenue without the MBB brand.*

---

## When to run this

You have access to a buyer who has budget, has a function worth transforming, and is willing to commit to a paid pilot. You want the pilot to convert. Run this playbook when you are scoping the opening engagement on a relationship you intend to grow.

This playbook does not apply when the buyer is asking for a one-off project, advisory only, or training. It applies when the goal is a multi-year relationship and the pilot is the wedge into it.

## Prerequisites

- A buyer with budget authority for the target function (or, ideally, a PE fund or holding company with authority over multiple operating units, see [PE fund cascade](/engagement-patterns/pe-fund-cascade)).
- A target function the buyer can point at and grade (sales, finance, operations, customer support).
- The willingness to do real fieldwork inside that function in the first month.

## Steps

1. **Sell the POC as paid, not free.** A free POC trains the buyer to undervalue the work. The POC pricing should sit between $9K/month at the floor and $30K+/month for fund-level retainers (the floor echoes the practitioner quoted in [pricing](/playbooks/pricing)'s Supporting voices; these numbers govern budget-holding mid-market and PE buyers, not the warm-SMB opener in [Pilot Pitch](/playbooks/pilot-pitch)). The buyer is paying for evidence, not for a demo.

2. **Interview every person who touches the function.** This is the foundational month. Not a sample. Not the team leads only. Every single person whose work flows through the function. The output is a workflow map and a universe map (see [pilot scope](/playbooks/pilot-scope)).

3. **Scope the transformation, then deploy inside a subset of the team.** Pick one squad, one geography, one product line. Deploy the AI workflow there. Let them use it for the back half of the POC. The point is generating evidence the buyer can point at, not boiling the ocean.

4. **Get one person measurably supercharged inside the first 45 days.** This is the proof artifact for the year-long upsell. By the end of month two, the buyer should be looking at a specific person on their team whose output has visibly stepped up. That is the case study you carry into the conversion conversation.

5. **At the end of the POC, propose the year-long engagement.** The scope is "roll out to everything." Same shape as the POC (interview, deploy, measure), now applied across the whole function. Pricing scales accordingly.

6. **Sell ROI, not effort.** The buyer is converting because the POC produced evidence. Anchor the upsell on the dollar impact of the supercharged person multiplied by the team size, the workflow lift quantified in hours per week, or the universe coverage as a strategic asset.

## Why this works

The buyer is paying for the POC, so the operator is not exposed. The POC produces real workflow lift and real evidence inside the buyer's org, so the conversion is on facts, not promises. The year-long engagement is structured the same way the POC was, so execution risk is low. And the universe-mapping artifact produced in month one is reusable across the rest of the function, which compounds the operator's leverage as the relationship scales.

The structure is copyable. The execution is the moat.
