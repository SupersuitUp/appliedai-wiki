# AI-Led Brand Interview

*A twelve-question protocol for surfacing the soul of a brand, project, or venture in a single session. Designed to be run by an AI interviewer with founders.*

---

This protocol pulls out the gut-level material that makes a brand specific instead of generic. It is built to be conducted by an AI interviewer with the founders or leaders in conversation. It works best with two or more people riffing together.

## Setup

- **Duration:** 45 to 90 minutes
- **Format:** conversational, not formal. The interviewer asks one question at a time and lets the founders riff. Capture everything.
- **Recording:** always record. Transcribe after.
- **Output:** raw interview notes saved immediately, then distilled into brand artifacts.

## The Questions, in Order

### 1. The Feeling

"When someone encounters [brand] for the very first time, what is the ONE feeling you want them to have before they understand anything about what you do? Not the explanation. The gut feeling."

*Why this question:* establishes the emotional foundation. Everything else builds on this.

### 2. The Space

"If [brand] were a physical space, what does it look like? Not an office. Close your eyes. What are you sitting on? What is the lighting? What sounds are there?"

*Why this question:* translates abstract brand feeling into sensory details that inform visual identity, event design, and spatial decisions.

### 3. The Anti-Position

"Who is NOT [brand]? Name a specific person, brand, or company that people might confuse you with, and tell me exactly why they are wrong."

*Why this question:* defining what you are not is often clearer than defining what you are. Surfaces the boundaries.

### 4. The Simple Version

"If you had to explain [brand] to someone who has never heard of your industry, how would you? Like talking to your grandmother or a kid."

*Why this question:* forces the founders past jargon into the core metaphor. Often produces the best brand language.

### 5. The Poison Words

"What are three words you would NEVER want associated with [brand]? Words that if someone used them to describe you, you would know they completely misunderstood."

*Why this question:* creates the anti-language list. Equally important as the language you DO use.

### 6. The Testimony

"When someone finishes working with [brand], what do they say about the experience to their friends? Not what you hope. What do they actually say, casually, over dinner?"

*Why this question:* reveals what the brand actually delivers vs. what it thinks it delivers. The gap between these is the brand's growth edge.

### 7. The Name

"What do you call the people you work with? Not the formal term. The word that feels right."

*Why this question:* language shapes relationship. "Client" creates distance. "Person" creates intimacy. The word chosen reveals the true power dynamic.

### 8. The Non-Negotiable

"What is the one thing [brand] will NEVER compromise on, even if it costs you money, reputation, or speed?"

*Why this question:* the non-negotiable is the brand's floor. Everything else can flex. This cannot.

### 9. The Archetype

"If [brand] had a patron figure, a mythical reference, or a story that embodies what you are trying to be, what is it?"

*Why this question:* connects the brand to something larger than itself. Often produces the deepest visual and narrative identity.

### 10. The 100 Mark

"What does [brand] look like at 100 people served? What has changed in the world?"

*Why this question:* forces concrete vision without the abstraction of massive scale. 100 is real enough to feel, big enough to matter.

### 11. The First Moment

"What is the first thing someone experiences when they start working with [brand]? The actual first moment of the work."

*Why this question:* tests whether the brand feeling (Q1) is actually delivered in practice. If Q1 and Q11 do not match, there is a gap to close.

### 12. The Motto

"If [brand] had a motto, something short enough to fit on a ring or coin, what would it be? Not a tagline. Something you would say to each other before walking into a room."

*Why this question:* distills everything into the smallest possible container. If the founders cannot say it in a few words, the brand is not clear yet.

## After the Interview

1. **Save raw notes immediately.** Do not wait. Context compresses fast.
2. **Let it sit overnight.** The best insights surface after sleeping on it.
3. **Distill into a brand guide.** Pull the throughlines. What kept coming back? If a theme appears in three different answers, that is the signal.
4. **Identify the anti-language.** Compile every poison word into a "Do NOT Use" list.
5. **Name the IP.** Did anything trademarkable emerge? Archetype systems, frameworks, coined terms.
6. **Write the philosophy page.** Core beliefs that surfaced belong at the organizational level, not buried in brand docs.

## Principles for the Interviewer

- **One question at a time.** Let them riff. Do not rush.
- **Capture the exact words.** The founders' natural language IS the brand language. Do not paraphrase.
- **Follow the energy.** If a question sparks a tangent, follow it. The tangent is often more valuable than the original question.
- **Note when answers repeat.** Repetition is signal. If the same theme keeps coming back across different questions, that is the core.
- **Do not lead.** Ask, then shut up. The silence is where the truth lives.
- **Save incrementally.** Do not rely on memory or context. Write to disk after every 2 to 3 answers.
