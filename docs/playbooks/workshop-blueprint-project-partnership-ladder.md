Most AI consultants try to sell a six-figure custom-build engagement on the first sales call. Mid-market buyers do not commit six figures to a vendor they met last week, no matter how good the pitch is. The work has to be earned in stages. The Workshop → Blueprint → Project → Partnership ladder, articulated by Devin Karns of Custom AI Studio, is the engagement structure that does the earning. Four fixed-price offers, each delivering real value, each producing the trust that opens the next door.

## What it produces

By the time a client has moved through all four steps:

- The consultant has been embedded in the client's strategic AI thinking for 6-18 months.
- The client has paid the consultant six to seven figures over the course of the ladder.
- The consultant has visibility into the client's full operations and can spot the next opportunity before any competitor.
- The engagement structure is contracted and recurring, which produces the kind of revenue that re-rates the firm's valuation (see [The Services-Firm Re-Rating Threshold](/perspectives/services-firm-re-rating-threshold)).

A client who completes all four steps is structurally hard for another firm to win.

## When to run it

- **From the first sales call onward** with any mid-market prospect ($10M-$250M annual revenue, see [Mid-Markets Are the AI Sweet Spot](/perspectives/mid-markets-are-the-ai-sweet-spot)).
- **As the standard sales path**, not the exception. The discount path (skipping straight to a custom project) is reserved for repeat clients or warm referrals where the prior steps have already been earned through prior work.

## The four steps

### Step 1: AI Workshop ($1-5k)

**Format:** Two sessions, totaling 3 hours.

**Session 1 (1 hour): Landscape briefing.** A presentation on the current state of AI for general audiences. What models exist, what they can and cannot do, where the field is going in the next 12 months, where the noise is coming from. Calibrated to the client's stated industry but generally applicable.

**Session 2 (2 hours): Industry-specific application.** Between session 1 and session 2, the client provides a set of pain points and operational questions specific to their business. Session 2 is a presentation of what AI could plausibly do for those specific pain points, calibrated to where the field is now.

**The deliverable.** A slide deck the client can take to their leadership team or board, framed as the consultant's expert assessment of "where AI fits in your business."

**Why this step earns the next one.** The client now trusts the consultant as the expert in the room and has alignment with the consultant on the current state of AI. They are unlikely to argue about basic facts on the next call. The price is low enough that procurement does not block it.

### Step 2: AI Blueprint ($15-35k)

**Format:** A 4-6 week discovery process with named milestones.

**The work.** Deep interviews with the client's operators, function heads, and IT/engineering team. Mapping current workflows. Identifying the highest-leverage AI opportunities, scoring each by ROI potential and feasibility. Designing the technical architecture for the top 1-3 opportunities. Producing a phased implementation plan with budget estimates.

**The deliverable.** A 30-50 page blueprint document the client could take to any dev shop and have them build against. Includes current-state analysis, target architecture, named opportunities with ROI math, implementation phasing, budget estimates, and risk register.

**Why this step earns the next one.** The client now has a real plan. They have invested real money in producing it. They are unlikely to take the plan to a competitor (the natural buyer for the build is the firm that wrote the blueprint). The blueprint also flushes out the actual budget for the build, which lets the client get internal alignment before the build kicks off.

**Pricing intuition.** Custom AI Studio charges $15-35k depending on company size and number of workflows. This is roughly McKinsey discovery-pricing for a smaller deliverable, calibrated to mid-market budgets.

### Step 3: Custom Project (variable, typically $50k-$500k)

**Format:** A fixed-scope build engagement, typically 3-6 months.

**The work.** Build whichever of the named opportunities from the blueprint the client has selected as their first investment. Architect, develop, integrate with the client's existing systems, test, deploy, train the client's team to operate it.

**The deliverable.** A working AI system, documentation, and a trained internal owner on the client's side.

**Why this step earns the next one.** The client has now seen the consultant deliver a real production system that produces real business outcomes. Trust is no longer hypothetical. The relationship has been tested against the build's inevitable problems and survived. The client is comfortable with the consultant's senior engineers and project managers. Subsequent expansions have low friction.

**Pricing intuition.** Time-and-materials pricing is increasingly the wrong shape (see [The AI Tech Partnership Revenue Model](/playbooks/ai-tech-partnership-revenue-model)). Custom AI Studio prices custom projects as fixed-scope deliverables tied to value, with milestone payments.

### Step 4: AI Technology Partnership ($200k-$2M+ annually)

**Format:** Multi-year ongoing engagement, typically structured as a budget plus performance fee.

**The work.** The consultant becomes the client's de facto AI department. Continuous discovery of new opportunities, continuous architecture work, continuous build, continuous operational support. The consultant's team is embedded with the client's leadership.

**The deliverable.** Ongoing strategic AI capability inside the client's organization, attributable to the consultant.

**Why this step is the goal.** Recurring revenue. Contracted commitment. The kind of relationship that re-rates the consulting firm's valuation. Also the most strategically interesting work because the consultant is now operating at the level of CEO advisory, not vendor delivery.

**Pricing intuition.** Budget plus a percentage of attributable top-line revenue (or attributable cost savings). 15% is the conventional growth-marketing-partner number; AI tech partnerships are still finding their pricing equilibrium. See [The AI Tech Partnership Revenue Model](/playbooks/ai-tech-partnership-revenue-model) for the full structure.

## How to position the ladder on a sales call

The instinct is to lead with the partnership. The right move is to lead with the workshop and let the prospect see the ladder.

```
"There are usually four ways our engagements evolve with mid-market clients.
The first is a workshop: a few thousand dollars, a few hours, and we get
on the same page about what AI can actually do for your industry. Most
clients start there. From there, if it makes sense, we go to a blueprint,
which is a real discovery deliverable you could take to any dev shop. Then
the actual build. Then, for some clients, an ongoing partnership. We don't
need to talk about steps 3 and 4 right now. Let's start with the workshop."
```

This positioning does three things at once: it lowers the perceived risk of step one, it gives the prospect a visible map of where the relationship could go, and it signals that the consultant is calm about pacing the relationship rather than desperate for the big check.

## Common failure modes

**Skipping the workshop.** Selling a $30k blueprint cold without the trust the workshop builds. Sometimes works, often produces buyer's remorse mid-blueprint.

**Letting the blueprint become a free pre-sale.** Treating the blueprint as discovery for the build rather than a standalone paid deliverable. The blueprint earns the build; it is not the build's prep work.

**Pricing the blueprint too low.** A $5k blueprint is not taken seriously by the client. The pricing is part of what makes the deliverable feel real. $15-35k is the calibrated band.

**Selling the partnership before the project is done.** The partnership has to be earned through the project. Floating it during the project phase reads as upsell pressure and damages trust.

**Optimizing for the project, not the partnership.** Most consultants treat the project as the goal and forget to position for the partnership. Every project decision should be made with the partnership in view.

## The artifact

The engagement contract for each step lives in the consulting firm's repository at `engagements/<client-slug>/<step-name>.md`. Each step has its own contract, its own SOW, its own pricing, and its own deliverable. The four contracts together form the client's full engagement history.
