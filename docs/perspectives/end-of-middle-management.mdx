# The End of Middle Management

*In an AI-native company, two roles matter: ICs who build and operate, and DRIs who own outcomes. Middle management exists to coordinate, and AI does coordination now. A greenfield claim, not a prescription for legacy orgs.*

## Scope upfront

This is a perspective on net-new orgs. We are not arguing that legacy enterprises should fire their middle managers next week; we are arguing that if you are building a company today, the org shape that emerges from first principles has only two roles in it, and you should think hard before adding a third. The argument was articulated by a YC partner in a 2026 Root Access talk and it is downstream of YC's specific operating context (small teams, capital-rich, no compliance gravity, no legacy systems). Take it as a blueprint, not a universal law.

## The two roles

**Individual Contributors (ICs).** Builders and operators. The people doing the work directly: writing the code, talking to the customers, running the experiments, shipping the artifacts. In an AI-native company, every IC is also augmented by agents, so the gap between "the IC's output" and "what a small team used to produce" closes dramatically.

**Directly Responsible Individuals (DRIs).** Named humans accountable for specific outcomes. Not a committee, not a group, not a process owner. One person, one outcome, one decision-maker. DRIs exist so the organization can move; without them, every decision becomes a meeting.

The two roles compose: a DRI is responsible for an outcome, ICs (human and agentic) do the work, the DRI ships. There is no coordinating layer between them because the coordination has been absorbed into the agentic harness.

## Why middle management goes away

Middle management exists to solve a coordination problem. Information flows up so leaders can decide. Decisions flow down so ICs can execute. Status flows back up so leaders can monitor. In the Roman-legion company (see [Roles to Workflows](/concepts/roles-to-workflows)), this is the load-bearing function of the middle layer.

AI dissolves this load-bearing function. Information flows directly into the company brain ([Legible Organization](/disciplines/legible-organization)). Decisions are made by named DRIs with full agentic context. Status is queryable in real time by anyone with permissions. The information-passing job that middle managers used to do is now done by software, faster and more accurately, and the human friction of telephone-game distortion goes away.

What does not go away: mentorship, judgment, accountability, taste, strategic decisions. Those collapse upward into the DRI layer and outward into ICs. They are not the middle manager's unique value-add; they were always shared across the org.

## Implications for net-new orgs

If you are building a company today, the YC partner's argument is to skip the middle layer entirely. Hire ICs who can also be DRIs when the role calls for it. Build the agentic harness early so coordination is not a bottleneck. Treat headcount additions in the middle layer as suspect; ask whether the work could be absorbed by better agents and clearer DRIs instead.

The hiring funnel shifts. You are not looking for "people-managers" or "team-leads"; you are looking for senior ICs who can own outcomes and lead the agentic teams under them. The career ladder shape changes too: vertical movement is from IC to DRI to founder-level DRI, not IC to manager to senior manager.

## Implications for legacy orgs (cautious)

The YC frame does not transfer cleanly. Legacy orgs have thousands of middle managers whose role spans information routing, performance management, change management, and political work. Dissolving that layer overnight is destructive. But the directional question is real: which middle-management functions are coordination work that an agent could do, and which are judgment work that should collapse into DRIs and ICs? Use the question as an audit lens, not a layoff plan.

## What this perspective is not

- **Not "fire all your managers."** It is a claim about the org shape that emerges from first principles in a greenfield build.
- **Not "humans are interchangeable with agents."** Humans are essential, but at the IC and DRI layer, not the coordination layer.
- **Not "no leadership."** Leadership intensifies at the DRI layer. The leader-as-workflow-PM frame (see [Leader as Workflow PM](/roles/leader-as-workflow-pm)) describes what that looks like in practice.
