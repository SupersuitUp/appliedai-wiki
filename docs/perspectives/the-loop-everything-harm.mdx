# The Loop-Everything Harm

*The "write loops, let it run, stop prompting" narrative causes downstream harm to developers and organizations. The advice benefits the seller. The practitioner absorbs the cost.*

---

## What the narrative says

The "loop-everything" narrative from AI lab evangelists runs roughly as follows: prompting is the old way. Writing loops that let AI figure out what to do is the new way. Your job is to set a goal, let it run to a win condition, and step back. Hand-coding is gross. Prompting is old-fashioned. You write the loop.

This narrative is delivered by people with free and unlimited access to the tokens they are telling others to burn at scale.

## Who absorbs the cost

When a team builds their process around "let the loop run," several things happen downstream:

Code ships without controls. If the loop is not tightly specified, the resulting code is a black box. Nobody on the team understands what it does or why. The next engineer who touches it has no context. Reviews happen after the fact, if at all.

Developers burn out. The humans in these workflows become monitors of something they do not understand, responsible for outcomes they cannot fully evaluate. That is a specific kind of professional exhaustion: the exhaustion of nominal accountability without actual agency.

Job anxiety compounds. The "coding is solved" narrative, combined with the "let the loop run" workflow, tells every developer that their craft has been commoditized. That their judgment is no longer the point. The people delivering this message have equity in the company selling the loop. The people receiving it are employees trying to stay employed.

## The asymmetry

The person advising "write loops, let it run" gets free tokens for their own development work. When they tell practitioners to spend ten thousand dollars a day on token consumption, they are making a recommendation that costs them nothing and may cost the practitioner a significant portion of their engineering budget.

This is not evidence of bad intent. It is evidence of a real incentive gap that should prompt practitioners to apply appropriate skepticism before restructuring their entire workflow around an executive's yapping session.

## What to do instead

The correct response to AI productivity gains is to leverage them with judgment, not to replace judgment with automation.

Use AI tools to accelerate the work that a skilled engineer can then evaluate and own. Build a process where the human understands what shipped and why. Preserve the craft depth that lets you catch failures at the edges of what AI does well.

The [Jagged Frontier](/perspectives/the-jagged-frontier) is the map. The model has regions where it is brilliant and regions where it breaks. Knowing which side of the border a given output falls on requires the same judgment the "loop-everything" narrative says is no longer needed. Without that judgment, you ship the broken regions without knowing it.

The practitioners who will compound are the ones who move fast where AI is strong and bring real expertise where it is not. That pairing requires maintaining the expertise. The loop-everything narrative tells you to let it atrophy. That is the harm.
