# Wisdom Is the Only Durable AI Defense

*Classical formation in virtue and wisdom is the only durable response to AI's degradation of human cognitive capacity. The argument is not anti-technology. It is pro-human-depth.*

---

## The convergence point

In a June 2026 conversation, John Vervaeke and Jonathan Pageau independently converged on the same conclusion. Vervaeke: "The philosophical project, meaning philosophy as a way of life, the cultivation of virtue and wisdom, has never been more needed and has never been more under threat by the same phenomena."

Pageau: "The need for human sages will become the only thing that will be valuable in the future because everything else can be automated."

The convergence matters because it arrives from different directions. Vervaeke is a cognitive scientist making an empirical argument: if AI degrades cognition (which the research now shows it does), the response is to cultivate cognition through means that do not degrade it. Pageau is a symbolist making a structural argument: the only thing that cannot be outsourced to an extension of human power is the internal formation that makes a person capable of using any extension well.

They land at the same place: classical education, wisdom formation, and the cultivation of virtue are not nostalgic preferences. They are practical infrastructure for a world where AI is pervasive.

## Why AI makes this more urgent, not less

The case for wisdom education is often made as a conservative reaction: protect the old things before AI sweeps them away. That framing misses the sharper point.

AI makes wisdom education more urgent because the cost of lacking it is increasing. When AI can produce fluent outputs indistinguishable from expert work, the person without the internal formation to evaluate those outputs cannot discriminate what is good from what is merely convincing. They become captive to whatever the model generates.

Pageau put it in terms of the skill ladder: "The ladder to learning a skill is being cut. All the menial things that people used to do to put their foot in the door and learn a skill, you know, those things can now be done by AI." If you never had to do the hard work of building internal competence, you have no internal structure from which to evaluate AI outputs in that domain.

Vervaeke's empirical ground: the Flynn effect (the long-standing rise in population IQ) reversed for the first time in recorded history, with measurable declines starting around 2013 with social media, and LLMs accelerating the trend. A generation without internal cognitive formation will not be able to evaluate whether the AI it depends on is hallucinating.

## What wisdom education actually means here

This is not an argument to ban AI in schools or to return to pre-technological education wholesale. The argument is about sequence and emphasis.

Formation in wisdom means: internalizing stories, principles, and reasoning patterns before externalizing them to AI. Reading primary sources, not AI summaries. Writing arguments, not prompting for them. Doing the calculation by hand before handing it to the calculator. Building the cognitive scaffold first, then deciding where AI can extend it without hollowing it out.

Pageau's framing from the Symbolic World Summit resonated here: "Now is the time to remember." Memorize scripture. Build memory palaces. Internalize the canonical texts. The process of internalization builds the very cognitive structure that makes the person capable of navigating an AI-saturated world with judgment rather than dependency.

## The opportunity for operators building with AI

This argument creates a clear differentiation space for operators and educators. The tools and environments that help people build internal formation, rather than replacing it, become the most valuable ones as AI commoditizes the outputs of low-formation work.

What does that look like in practice? AI that asks questions rather than giving answers. AI that surfaces examples for the human to evaluate rather than making the evaluation. AI that trains discrimination, not generation. The [comparative human edge](/perspectives/comparative-human-edge) sharpens in proportion to the depth of the formation underneath it.

> **In a world where AI can generate anything, the only durable advantage is the judgment to know what is worth generating.**
