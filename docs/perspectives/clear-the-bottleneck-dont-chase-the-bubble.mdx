# Clear the Bottleneck, Don't Chase the Bubble

*Applied AI pays off fastest inside a business that has already proven its market fit, where it clears bottlenecks and compounds profit. Spinning up a speculative new AI product in today's crowded, bubbly market is a far worse use of the same energy.*

*By the Chairman.*

There are two very different things people mean when they say they are "doing AI" in 2026. One is applying AI to a business that already works. The other is trying to build a new AI business from scratch. They are not close in expected value, and right now the gap is wide enough that I will say it plainly: apply it where fit is already proven, and stay out of the bubble.

## Apply it where fit is already proven

The highest-leverage place to put AI is a business that has already earned its market. Proven fit means there is a real machine making real money, with real customers, real distribution, and real constraints. AI dropped into that machine has something to act on immediately. You are not hunting for product-market fit. You are removing the things that cap a machine that has already found it.

This is also why the mid-market is the sweet spot: those companies are systematized enough to build against, and close enough to their own P&L to feel the result the next week. See [Mid-Markets Are the AI Sweet Spot](/perspectives/mid-markets-are-the-ai-sweet-spot).

## The bubble I will not endorse

Speculatively building a new AI SaaS product is a different game, and a much harder one. The space is crowded and bubbly. Even the most successful AI-native companies are operating under enormous pressure, with real constraints on whether their valuations and revenue can keep their current trajectory. I would not chase a hot AI stock right now, and I would not start a random AI startup right now either. The energy it takes to go find fit is energy you could spend compounding a business that already has it.

## What applied AI actually does: clear the bottleneck

Here is the part that should get your attention if you already run something real. Applied AI is a revenue and profit maximizer. It clears bottlenecks for operators who are already making more money than they can operationally handle, so they can make more, and build the capacity to handle making even more. The constraint was never demand. The constraint was execution: the speed of translating decisions into shipped work. That is exactly what applied AI removes. See [Strategy Is the New Execution](/perspectives/strategy-is-the-new-execution).

## Who this is for

If you are an operator with a business that has clearly started, gained traction, and is now focused on scale, this is your golden age. The move is to apply AI to what already works, clear the bottleneck, and let the profit compound. If you are still searching for fit, AI will not manufacture it for you. Find the fit first. Then come back and remove the cap.
