# A Startup Is an Outcome Generator

*A startup is a company whose product reliably produces a valued outcome on demand. Accelerators are factories that manufacture outcome generators, and the frontier labs are the power source underneath all of them.*

## What a startup actually is now

Strip a startup down to its function and you find a machine: raw input goes in, a specific valued outcome comes out, reliably and on demand. That is an [outcome generator](/concepts/the-outcome-economy). Seen this way, the pitch a founder makes stops being "we are a software company in this category" and becomes "we own the generator for this outcome." The product is the generator. Revenue is what people pay to run it.

This reframes the whole enterprise. A [services firm that turns its business logic into an AI system](/concepts/ai-native-services-company) is building an outcome generator out of what used to be billable hours. A founder who [rebuilds a process around the intelligence instead of wrapping the old one](/perspectives/dont-get-attached-to-your-process) is building a better generator than the incumbent whose process is frozen. The unit of competition is the generator, and owning a good one is durable because the intelligence inside it is rented by everyone while the deployment is yours.

## The accelerator is a factory for outcome generators

If a startup is an outcome generator, then an accelerator is a factory that manufactures them. **Y Combinator** takes founders and raw ideas and stamps out a batch of companies on a repeatable process, twice a year, at scale. Techstars, the growing crop of venture studios, and the operator-led incubators do the same with different tooling. Their function reframes the same way a startup's does: not "we fund software companies" but "we manufacture outcome generators."

One precision keeps the layers honest. The accelerator does not produce the outcome. It produces the companies that ship the generators. The startup is the vehicle, the generator is the product, and the outcome is what the generator yields. A factory of vehicles that each contain a factory of outcomes.

## Outcome generators all the way up

The pattern nests. The factory is itself an outcome generator, one level up: its outcome is a funded, shaped startup, produced on a process it refines batch over batch. Go up again and a fund that manufactures accelerators is a generator whose outcome is factories. Go down and a single agent inside a product is a generator whose outcome is one completed task. It is outcome generators all the way up and all the way down, each one embedding intelligence to turn an input into a reliable result, each one someone else's building block.

This is why the startup-factory idea has teeth: helping one capable person spin up a net-new company with agents is building a personal outcome-generator factory. The leverage is not in producing one outcome. It is in producing the thing that produces outcomes.

## Where the frontier labs sit

Every generator in this stack runs on intelligence it does not make itself. A handful of frontier labs, **Anthropic** among them, build the superintelligence that outcome generators embed. No lab will build every narrow application on top of it, so the narrow generators get built by thousands of specialized businesses, each turning one domain of expertise into one reliable outcome, on the platform.

That gives a lab like Anthropic a specific structural position in the outcome economy. It supplies the power source, it enables the ecosystem of generators built on top, and through [consumption](/perspectives/the-token-economy) it captures a share of the value every generator produces. The larger move is one level up from any single generator: a lab can shepherd not just the generators but the factories that mass-produce them, the accelerators and studios and agencies and evangelist programs that turn capable people into companies that ship generators. Powering one founder at a time is a market. Powering the factories that make founders is a much larger one.
