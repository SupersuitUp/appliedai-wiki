## Why time-for-money breaks

A full year of knowledge-worker labor (lawyer, designer, professor, editor, consultant) can now be reproduced in roughly $200,000 of AI tokens, and the token cost is falling. Any business whose invoices read in hourly rates, day rates, or monthly retainers is competing against a substitute that is two orders of magnitude cheaper and improving every quarter.

> "If you have a business that requires you to sit at your desk, looking at a screen, going through emails, you are absolutely selling time for money. If I look at your invoices and you are selling day rates or monthly retainers or hourly rates, you are selling time for money."
>
> Daniel Priestley, ["Why You Must Stop Selling Your Time for Money"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkPatBreE9w), YouTube, 2026-05-23

This is the same arithmetic that drives [The Token Economy](/perspectives/the-token-economy) and (from the principal side) [The Token-Profit Squeeze](https://agenticbusiness.wiki/concepts/the-token-profit-squeeze). The conclusion for the implementer is direct: the billable hour is the wrong unit. The work has to be repackaged.

Priestley's contribution is the repackaging frame. He names five categories of human contribution that AI does not replace, that compound rather than collapse, and that buyers will actually pay for once the time-for-money frame breaks.

## The five things you actually get paid for

**1. Conviction.** What you want to see happen in the world. Not a preference, not a take. The thing your life force pushes toward whether or not it is convenient. AI has no conviction. It mirrors the user back to the user and tells them what they want to hear. The market is starved for people who actually want a specific outcome and will act on that want.

> "AI doesn't have any conviction. It will tell you anything you want to hear. Every single person who's using AI will tell you that the AI is a total sycophant."
>
> Daniel Priestley, ["Why You Must Stop Selling Your Time for Money"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkPatBreE9w), YouTube, 2026-05-23

Conviction without action evaporates. Priestley's frame: conviction is a flow that finds the people who act on it. Hesitate too long and it routes around you.

**2. Judgment.** Given the conviction, the choices that move it forward. Priorities, paths, tools, teams, sequencing. Judgment is built from being wrong in public enough times to become reliably right. See [The Judgment Line](/perspectives/the-judgment-line) for the implementer-side discipline.

**3. Taste.** Creative choices that resonate with a specific buyer. Different markets want different things, and the operator with taste is the one in close contact with the actual customers, picking up the signals AI cannot pick up from training data alone.

**4. Commercial outcomes.** Sales and delivery. Priestley frames the entire field as two problems, and only two: **speed to value** (the customer's outcome arrives faster than alternatives) and **speed to market** (the customer learns you exist faster than alternatives). Everything else is noise. The operator gets paid for moving the needle on these two, regardless of whether the underlying work was done by human or machine.

**5. Improving the system.** Tinkering with the production stack so speed to value and speed to market both compound. New tools, new models, better data, better routing. The operator who runs the system improvement loop owns the curve; the operator who runs the same stack month after month loses the curve.

Priestley's summary, in his own structure:

> "Conviction is why do we do this. Judgment is what are we going to do. Taste is who do we do this for and how do we connect with them. Commercial success is about when do we land this plane. And systems is how do we do it better."
>
> Daniel Priestley, ["Why You Must Stop Selling Your Time for Money"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wkPatBreE9w), YouTube, 2026-05-23

## Digital soil

The shape Priestley sees in the economy is the agricultural one repeating. In the agricultural age, the farmer did roughly two acts on each end of the season (decide when to plant, plant; decide when to harvest, harvest and sell) while the land automated the six steps in the middle. The land was, in Priestley's phrase, an old kind of artificial intelligence: you put in a seed (a prompt), and a bushel of wheat came out.

The post-AI economy runs the same shape on digital soil. The operator decides what to start, sets the brief, leaves the middle to the system, decides when it is done, and brings it to market. This is the same role split [Humans As Instigators And Editors](/perspectives/humans-as-instigators-and-editors) names from a different angle: the two human anchors hold the work, the agent fills the middle.

## What this means for the Applied AI consultant

For the consultant audience, the five-things framing does double duty.

**Inward.** It is a mirror. A consulting practice that bills hours, packages retainers, and scopes deliverables in time units is doing exactly what Priestley's video tells the client to stop doing. The repricing pressure that hits the client hits the consultant first, because the consultant is closer to the substitution frontier. See [Pricing](/playbooks/pricing) for the practical repackaging moves: outcome-priced engagements, productized offers, equity-and-revenue structures, retainers that buy access to judgment rather than hours of execution.

**Outward.** It is a diagnostic for the engagement. When advising a principal, the five-things frame surfaces which of the client's revenue-producing activities are actually defensible. Conviction, judgment, taste, commercial wins, and system improvements are the parts of the client's work that survive. Tasks that look like billable execution are the parts that compress to tokens. The consultant's job in the engagement is to help the principal move their team out of the executing band and into the bookend bands.

The consultant who internalizes this frame builds practices that look more like [The Agent Is The Increment](/perspectives/the-agent-is-the-increment) and less like a body-shop. The consultant who does not internalize it competes on day-rate with a substitute that does not sleep.
