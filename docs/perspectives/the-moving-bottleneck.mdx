# The Moving Bottleneck

*Make one layer cheap and the binding constraint does not disappear. It relocates. Efficiency is a frontier you keep advancing, not a finish line you cross once.*

---

## The pattern

Every system has one constraint that decides its cost or its speed. Relieve that constraint and the system gets cheaper or faster, but only until the next constraint becomes the one that binds. The bottleneck moved. It did not vanish.

This is old wisdom in operations (Goldratt's theory of constraints names it directly), and it is the single most useful lens for reading what frontier AI labs are actually doing. The labs that win are not the ones who optimize one thing brilliantly. They are the ones who keep finding the next bottleneck and attacking it before anyone else notices it became the constraint.

## DeepSeek V4 as the worked example

DeepSeek V4 is the cleanest recent illustration. The team set one question and refused to let go of it: how do you make a million-token context actually affordable? Watch the constraint move as they answer it.

1. **Attention is the first bottleneck.** A standard KV cache grows one entry per token, so a million tokens means a million memory slots to read. DeepSeek attacks this with two new attention mechanisms that compress the cache and read it sparsely, cutting the per-token cost of long context dramatically.

2. **Expert weights become the next bottleneck.** Once attention is cheap, a large part of the serving cost for a sparse mixture-of-experts model is moving and computing expert weights. So they train those weights in FP4, low enough precision that the model learns to survive it during inference.

3. **Precision of the indexer moves next.** With attention and experts handled, they push even the attention indexer into lower precision. The constraint keeps relocating, and they keep chasing it.

The result reads, in bycloud's words, "like an engineering system paper as much as a model paper." No single trick carried it. The whole stack was redesigned around one binding question, and each fix surfaced the next thing to fix.

## Why this matters for operators

The moving bottleneck is not a model-lab curiosity. It is the shape of every efficiency project you will run.

- **A win reveals the next constraint, so plan for it.** When you make one step of a workflow cheap or fast, the slow part of the pipeline relocates. Instrument for where cost goes next instead of declaring victory.
- **Hold one binding question, not a list of optimizations.** DeepSeek's coherence came from refusing to optimize ten things and instead asking one question ten times. A scattered efficiency effort optimizes whatever is easy. A disciplined one keeps re-aiming at whatever currently binds.
- **The constraint determines the leverage.** Effort spent on a non-binding part of the system produces no end-to-end gain. Find the real constraint first, then spend there. This is why [strategy is the new execution](/perspectives/strategy-is-the-new-execution): knowing which bottleneck to attack is worth more than attacking quickly.

## The closing frame

Efficiency is not a state you reach. It is a frontier you advance along, one binding constraint at a time. The operators and labs that compound are the ones who treat every win as a question (where did the cost just go?) rather than an answer.
