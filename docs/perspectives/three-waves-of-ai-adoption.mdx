# Three Waves of AI Adoption

*AI adoption follows the same three-wave shape every prior general-purpose technology took. Wave 1 squeezes efficiency from existing workflows. Wave 2 raises quality. Wave 3 invents new categories. Wave 1 success does not predict Wave 3 success.*

---

## The three waves

The framing comes from Board of Innovation's strategy work with large corporates. It generalizes the adoption pattern observed in electricity, digital, and mobile, and applies it to AI.

**Wave 1, efficiency.** The technology is applied to the existing way of working. The questions are about time savings, cost savings, and overall productivity. Most current corporate AI spend lives here: chat assistants for support, AI-drafted emails, AI-summarized documents. The output looks identical to the pre-AI version, only faster and cheaper.

**Wave 2, quality.** The technology is used to produce better output, not just faster output. The questions shift from "how do we do this faster" to "what could we do that we could not do before." The output is recognizably better than the pre-AI version.

**Wave 3, new systems.** The technology enables entirely new categories of product, business model, or workflow. The questions are "what kind of company exists in this world that did not exist before." Digital's wave 3 created Uber, Airbnb, and Netflix. AI's wave 3 is still being invented.

## Why Wave 1 success does not predict Wave 3 success

BOI's sharpest argument: the investments and capabilities that win Wave 1 do not necessarily carry over to Wave 3. Wave 1 rewards process automation and incremental optimization. Wave 3 rewards strategic imagination, new business models, and the willingness to cannibalize existing revenue streams.

A company that masters AI-assisted customer support has not learned how to redesign customer relationships around continuous agentic interaction. A company that builds great AI-drafted reports has not learned how to ship products whose entire production pipeline is autonomous.

The implication for strategy: plan a portfolio across all three waves, with a Wave 3 vision as the anchor. Wave 1 and Wave 2 investments should be evaluated by whether they build toward the Wave 3 vision, not by their standalone ROI.

## A worked example, video

BOI uses video generation as a worked example:

- Wave 1: use AI to edit video faster and cheaper.
- Wave 2: use AI to produce higher-quality video than human editors alone could produce.
- Wave 3: real-time movie generation for an audience of one. Your personalized stream. Your personalized ad. Your personalized show, generated in the moment.

A company that wins Wave 1 in video editing is not automatically positioned to win Wave 3, where the production model is entirely different. Wave 1 capability is process; Wave 3 capability is real-time personalization at scale.

## How to use this framing in client work

When auditing an organization's AI roadmap, plot each initiative against the three waves and look at the distribution. A roadmap that is 95% Wave 1 with no Wave 3 vision is a sign that the organization is treating AI as a productivity tool, not a strategic shift. A roadmap that is heavy on Wave 3 imagination but light on Wave 1 wins will not survive the next budget cycle. The healthy distribution has investments in all three with a clear Wave 3 anchor.
