## How it works

A practitioner writes one file: a [GENERATE.md](/reference/standards/generate-md) recipe that scaffolds a single artifact. They hand it to a prospect over email, a message, or a link. The prospect pastes it into their own AI harness, the kind that renders [HTML-first artifacts](/concepts/html-first-artifacts).

The recipe does not dump a form. It runs a short, warm interview. It asks the prospect about their business in plain language, one question at a time, and invites them to paste or upload whatever context they already have: a website, a deck, a pricing page, a description of a painful week. The prospect answers in their own words. The interview feels like a conversation with someone curious about their work.

Then the harness renders. Out comes a clickable deck built for that one business: the specific workflows AI could take over, the hours it could return, the new offers it could unlock, all in the prospect's own language and, where the recipe carries an [Agentic Brand OS](/concepts/agentic-brand-os), on the prospect's own brand. The last slide is a warm introduction to the practitioner and an offer to help make it real.

The prospect experiences it alone, in minutes, by pasting one file. Nothing was scheduled. Nobody presented. The artifact is theirs to keep and to forward to a partner or a board.

## Why it beats the audit and the generic funnel

Nobody wants to be audited. An [audit](/playbooks/audit-to-sprint-to-deployment) is a valuable engagement once trust exists, but as a first touch it casts the prospect as a subject with problems to be exposed. The Generative Welcome casts them as the owner of a business worth getting excited about. It is a gift, not an examination.

A generic funnel sends everyone the same landing page, the same demo, the same PDF. The prospect has to do the translation work themselves: read the generic promise, then imagine how it maps onto their business. Most never finish the translation, so the aha never lands. The Generative Welcome does the translation for them and shows the mapped result. The abstract claim "AI could transform your business" becomes a concrete artifact about their business.

It also inverts who does the pitching. Instead of the practitioner pushing a narrative, the prospect generates the narrative about their own operation and reads it in their own words. That is the exact shift behind [Buying Relief, Not AI](/perspectives/buying-relief-not-ai): the prospect leaves holding a coherent story they can take to their board, and the practitioner is the guide who handed them the recipe that produced it.

## When to use it

Reach for the Generative Welcome at the top of a relationship, before any engagement is scoped:

- A cold or warm prospect who has heard "AI could help you" and felt nothing concrete.
- An inbound lead you want to qualify and warm at the same time, without a discovery call.
- A community, cohort, or list where a single shared recipe lets hundreds of people each self-serve a personalized result.
- Any moment where the goal is a first aha, not a signed statement of work.

It is a first touch, not a deliverable. Once the prospect is warm and wants the real thing, the engagement moves into the [Transformation Arc](/perspectives/the-transformation-arc): the ROI-ranked blueprint, the quick wins, the sequenced rollout. The Welcome earns the right to that conversation.

## The ingredients

Four parts make a Generative Welcome work:

1. **The recipe.** A [GENERATE.md](/reference/standards/generate-md) file that scaffolds exactly one artifact and stays idle afterward. It carries the interview questions, the artifact template, and the rules for turning answers into slides.
2. **The interview.** A short, delightful, one-question-at-a-time interview that also accepts pasted or uploaded context. This is the same mechanic as the [Boomerang Prompt](/concepts/boomerang-prompt), turned around: the boomerang returns raw material to the builder, while the Welcome returns a finished artifact to the person answering.
3. **The artifact.** A beautiful, clickable, [HTML-first artifact](/concepts/html-first-artifacts) the prospect keeps and shares. Where brand fidelity matters, the recipe carries an [Agentic Brand OS](/concepts/agentic-brand-os) so the output lands on the prospect's own brand.
4. **The introduction.** A warm, human close: who the practitioner is, what they do, and a low-friction offer to help build what the artifact imagined.

Packaged together, the recipe is a [Playable Harness Experience](/concepts/playable-harness-experience): designed once, shared as a file, played by anyone whose harness can load it. One practitioner writes it, and every prospect who pastes it gets a private, personalized welcome.
