## The claim

The most valuable thing a world-class practitioner owns is not their hours. It is the accumulated judgment inside those hours: how the top radiologist reads an ambiguous scan, how the best real-estate operator in a market prices a deal, how a veteran recruiter screens a hundred resumes down to three. Until recently the only ways to sell that judgment were hours (consulting), content (courses, books), or headcount (train someone). All three cap out. Hours do not scale, content does not act, and trained people leave.

A digital employee is the fourth option: the expert's judgment encoded into an agentic system that customers rent. Not a chatbot that talks about the domain, but a worker that does the work the way the expert does it, built on an agent SDK, running on the expert's rules, checklists, decision criteria, and taste. The customer is not hiring the expert. They are renting an employee the expert trained once and now leases many times over.

The category has a secondary name: **SMEaaS**, subject-matter expertise as a service. The two names describe the same product from opposite ends. "Digital employee" says what the thing does day to day: it shows up and works a seat. "SMEaaS" says what the customer is actually buying: a practitioner's hard-won expertise, delivered as a service instead of a salary. Use whichever frame the room needs.

## Why this category is new

The pieces did not exist together before. Encoding judgment used to mean years of bespoke software engineering, which only made sense at enterprise scale. Agent SDKs collapsed that cost: the harness, the tool use, the loop are commodity infrastructure now, so the only scarce input left is the expertise itself. That inverts who can build the product. The moat is not the engineering; it is the twenty years of pattern recognition the expert pours into the [rules and context](/concepts/agent-rule-files) the agent runs on.

This is also why the category passes [the Sam Altman test](/concepts/the-sam-altman-test). A smarter base model makes a digital employee stronger, not obsolete, because the durable value is the proprietary judgment and the operational packaging, not the raw inference. The model is the engine. The expert supplied the driver.

## Digital employee vs. seat wrapper

The two are easy to confuse and are opposite motions. A [seat wrapper](/concepts/seat-wrapper) points inward: it runs the repeatable roles of a seat inside your own business, so your human keeps the edge work. A digital employee points outward: it packages a human's edge itself into something other businesses rent. The seat wrapper is an efficiency asset. The digital employee is a product line, often the first genuinely scalable revenue a services expert has ever owned.

The progression between them is natural. An expert who [documents and streamlines their load-bearing workflows](/perspectives/document-and-streamline-load-bearing-workflows) has already externalized most of what a digital employee needs. The step from "my seat runs on my encoded judgment" to "your company rents my encoded judgment" is packaging, pricing, and a boundary: which decisions the digital employee makes alone, and which get escalated to a human.

## What it takes to build one honestly

- **Real expertise, made explicit.** The build is mostly interviews and worked examples, not code: extracting the decision criteria the expert applies without noticing. An expert who cannot articulate their judgment yet needs the [hyperdocumented SOP](/concepts/hyperdocumented-sop) discipline first.
- **A scoped job, not a persona.** "A digital employee that does X" beats "an AI version of me." Scope it like a hire: one seat's worth of responsibility, with defined inputs, outputs, and escalation rules.
- **The expert stays the source of truth.** The judgment keeps evolving, so the product needs a maintenance loop where the expert reviews edge cases and the encoded rules get sharper. A digital employee abandoned by its expert decays into a wrapper.
- **Honest limits.** The [judgment line](/perspectives/the-judgment-line) still applies. The digital employee runs the systematizable majority of the work; the irreducibly human calls stay human, and saying so is what makes buyers trust the rest.

## Why it matters economically

For the expert, this is the first product where their expertise earns while they sleep, without the quality collapse of hiring imitators. For the ecosystem, it is where serious [token consumption](/perspectives/the-token-economy) actually lives: a rented digital employee working inside many customer businesses consumes orders of magnitude more inference than the expert's own seat ever would. The interesting AI businesses are increasingly this shape, expertise at the top, agentic infrastructure underneath, consumption scaling with customers rather than with headcount.
