# Anxious Creativity

*The compulsion to be doing something with AI without knowing what. It reads as creativity and feels like progress, but it is anxiety: a fear of falling behind that pulls you off the proven work you already do well and into an endless churn of new tools, new apps, and half-built experiments.*

## The tell

**Anxious Creativity** shows up as a low hum of obligation. "I should be doing something with AI." "I should be on the new tool everyone posted about." "Maybe I need Obsidian. Maybe I need a command center. Maybe I need to be running Claude Code every night." None of these thoughts start from a workflow that hurts. They start from the feeling that other people are moving and you are not.

The result is motion without a target. You open a new app, wire up a new experiment, watch a demo, install a framework, and at the end of the day you have touched a lot of AI and moved nothing that makes money. The activity was real. The leverage was zero.

## Doom-creating, not doom-scrolling

AI has become the new social media, and Anxious Creativity is social media at its worst pointed inward. The old failure mode was the doom-scroll: passive, numbing, free. The new one is the doom-create: active, stimulating, and expensive. You are not consuming other people's noise. You are producing your own, burning tokens and hours to feel like you are keeping up.

The doom-create is more dangerous precisely because it feels productive. Scrolling at least admits it is a vice. Building a fourth half-finished tool wears the costume of progress, so nobody stops you, least of all yourself. This is the same mechanism [Idolizing the Build](/perspectives/idolizing-the-build) describes on the developer's side. Anxious Creativity is the version that catches operators and owners who cannot even code: the object of the anxiety is not the craft of building, it is the fear of standing still.

## It is a trust problem

Underneath the churn is a failure of trust. You do not actually believe that ignoring this week's trend is safe, so you react to all of it. The antidote is not another tool. It is the confidence that the proven thing you already do well is enough to build on, and that you do not have to chase every new capability the day it ships.

The people who get the most out of AI are usually the calmest about it. They are not early to everything. They are focused on the few workflows that already make them money, and they let the noise pass. When you stop trying to do something with AI and start trying to make your existing work cheaper and better, the anxiety drains out and the leverage shows up.

## The cure is boring

The exit from Anxious Creativity is not a better tool or a bigger token budget. It is a change of posture: [Progressive Automation Is the Job](/perspectives/progressive-automation-is-the-job). Map one proven, money-making workflow. Automate the parts that should be automated. Do it again next week. The work is unglamorous, and that is the point. Boring, repeatable automation of things that already pay is the opposite of anxious churn, and it is the only version that compounds.
