## The claim

[The Jagged Frontier](/concepts/the-jagged-frontier) maps AI's side of the line: an uneven, moving border between what models do brilliantly and what they botch. The comparative human edge is the sibling map on the other side. It marks the capacities where a person stays decisively better than the model, and where that gap actually decides whether a role succeeds.

Both halves are needed to design a seat that AI cannot fill. The frontier tells you what to hand the model. The comparative human edge tells you what to protect for the human, and therefore who the seat needs. Where the jagged frontier names the region where AI is irreplaceable, this names the region where the person is.

## It is the roles in a seat that fall outside the frontier

The ontology here is [EOS](https://traction.wiki), the Entrepreneurial Operating System from Gino Wickman's *Traction*, documented in full at traction.wiki. In EOS a [seat](https://traction.wiki/concepts/seat) is not a single role. A seat is one position with a clear success purpose, made up of several [roles](https://traction.wiki/concepts/five-major-roles): the major responsibilities the person in it owns. So you do not score the comparative human edge for a person in general, or even for a whole seat as one blob. You break the seat into its roles and plot each role onto [the jagged frontier](/concepts/the-jagged-frontier).

Some roles land inside the frontier, where the model is already strong. Some land outside it, where a person stays decisively better and the gap is load-bearing for the seat's purpose. The comparative human edge is that second set: the roles in this seat that sit outside the frontier. Reading a room is one role inside a high-ticket-closing seat and barely present in a data-reconciliation seat, which is why you map per seat and per role, against what the seat is measured on.

Because the frontier moves outward, roles cross from outside to inside over time. The edge narrows, and the person concentrates on fewer, higher roles. Re-plot the seat as the border advances.

## Where the edge concentrates

The edge clusters in a few recognizable places. It is strongest where a relationship carries the value, where real money rides on a single judgment, where a person is trusting someone with their safety, and where the point is partly the human presence itself.

- **High-ticket sales.** Selling expensive, high-consideration things (large real-estate and land deals, premium engagements, anything where the buyer needs to trust a person before moving a lot of money) stays human because the trust is the product.
- **Stakes-on-a-life roles.** People do not hand their physical safety to an unproven machine. An instructor for a dangerous activity, and for now a surgeon, hold the edge because the cost of the model being wrong on the wrong side of the frontier is a life.
- **Presence-as-the-point service.** A segment of buyers wants a human in the loop because the human is part of what they are buying: the guide, the host, the person who handles the situation no script anticipated.

The honest cut: humans stay load-bearing most reliably in the roles where a lot of money or a lot of consequence is on the line. That is where buyers refuse the commodity version.

## The premium inverts the hype

As the model gets cheaper at the commodity version of a task, the human version does not vanish. It moves up-market and becomes the premium tier. A segment of buyers, often the ones with the most money, will pay more precisely because a capable person is doing it. They want a guide who carries the weight, not a self-service flow.

So the durable pattern is two tiers, not one. AI-only is the budget lane for buyers who would rather not deal with a person. The human-wielding-AI version is the premium lane for buyers who want the edge and will pay for it. The hype says AI replaces the role. The boring, load-bearing truth is that AI splits the role into a cheap automated tier and an expensive human one.

## Designing seats around the edge

For an implementer, this is the rule that keeps a build honest. Do not try to automate the comparative human edge. Find it, staff the seat with someone whose gifts match it, and build the AI to carry the rest.

That is the job of a [Seat Wrapper](/concepts/seat-wrapper): it runs the roles that fell inside the frontier so the person spends their hours on the roles that fell outside, the narrow band where they are the reason the seat wins. Point the automation at the in-frontier roles and point the human at the out-of-frontier ones. A build that aims the model at the edge itself produces the premium role's cheap impersonation, which is the one thing the premium buyer is paying not to get.
