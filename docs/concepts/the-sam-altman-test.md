# The Sam Altman Test

*As the models get better, does your service get stronger, or does the model itself commoditize you? Build only where capability jumps are tailwinds.*

---

Every business built on top of frontier models faces one structural question: what happens to you when the next model ships. The Sam Altman test is YC's one-line way of forcing that question early, before you have committed years to a market. Ask: as the models get better, does my service get *stronger*, or does the model itself commoditize *me*?

You want to be in the first camp. There, every capability jump is a tailwind. A smarter base model lowers your cost of delivery, widens the range of work you can absorb, and extends your lead over slower-moving incumbents who are not built to absorb the improvement. The model is an engine you keep upgrading under a business whose value lives somewhere the model cannot reach: the domain relationships, the regulatory standing, the operational machine, the accumulated context.

In the second camp, the model *is* your product, thinly wrapped. When the next version arrives, it does for free what you charged for, and your moat evaporates. You were renting your business from a lab and did not know it.

## How to tell which camp you're in

The tell is where your durable value sits. If a capability jump erases your edge, your edge was the capability, and you are exposed. If a capability jump *amplifies* your edge, your edge was something else (a hard-won market, an operation, a compliance moat, a body of proprietary context) and the model is leverage on top of it.

This is the same logic as [open weights as a price ceiling](/perspectives/open-weights-as-price-ceiling): anything a commodity model can do, a commodity model will eventually do at commodity prices. Build above that line.

For an [AI-native services company](/concepts/ai-native-services-company), the test usually passes, because the value lives in the operation and the market position, not in the raw inference. But it is worth running deliberately on any market before you commit, and re-running it every time the frontier moves.
