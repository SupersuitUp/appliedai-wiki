# Curriculum

*The module sequence for taking an operator from "AI is happening" to a working personal AI operating environment.*

---

This is the curriculum spine. It is organized to take a non-technical operator through three layers: why this moment matters, the vocabulary and mental models, and the implementation. The arc is deliberate. Most attempts at AI literacy skip the foundation and go straight to tools, which produces operators who can run a prompt but cannot build a system. This sequence does the foundation work first.

## Part 0: Why This Moment Matters

The philosophical foundation. Why AI changes what people are FOR, not just what they can DO. This is the section that earns the operator's attention before any setup begins.

- **0.1 The Great Reveal.** Why AI changes what people are FOR, not just what they can DO.
- **0.2 What Is Agency?** High agency in the ordinary sense, then what it takes to operate at a level current frameworks do not yet describe.
- **0.3 What Is Compound Agency?** Purpose-driven action executed at scale by technology and aligned with the operator's articulated will.
- **0.4 What Is Applied AI?** The difference between knowing AI exists and applying it to your specific life.

## Part 1: The Foundation (Concepts)

Vocabulary and mental models before touching tools. Each module asks "what is this?" and "why does it matter for my work?"

- **1.1 What Is an AI Agent?** Software that acts on your behalf with goals and tools.
- **1.2 What Is Prompt Engineering?** Clear thinking disguised as AI communication.
- **1.3 What Is Context Engineering?** The unlock most people miss: better context beats better questions.
- **1.4 What Is Markdown?** Human-readable AND machine-readable writing.
- **1.5 What Is Git and Version Control?** A time machine for your life's work.
- **1.6 What Are Workflows, Skills, and Automations?** Build once, runs forever, gets better.
- **1.7 What Is Security and Why Does It Matter?** Sovereignty over your data is sovereignty over your work.

## Part 2: The Build (Implementation)

Build the personal AI operating environment piece by piece. Templates, walkthroughs, reference setups.

- **2.1 The Command Center.** Define pillars, write your agent's master instructions file, create a principles file.
- **2.2 Build Your Personal Context Profile.** A scored, living document of the operator's natural advantages: gifting fit, mission alignment, context leverage, real-world impact. Threshold: a 3-out-of-4 score on three dimensions means an opportunity is aligned with your advantage.
- **2.3 The Personal Relationship Manager.** A structured system for stewarding every relationship.
- **2.4 Skills That Compound.** Create reusable instruction sets for your agent.
- **2.5 Agent Automation Tiers.** Three tiers of agent autonomy:
  - *Static* documents that the agent reads but does not run.
  - *Triggered* workflows that the agent runs when you ask.
  - *Autonomous* loops that the agent runs on a schedule or in response to events, without prompting.
  Daily briefings, weekly reviews, monthly recalibrations, drift detectors all live in the autonomous tier.
- **2.6 The Artifact System.** Everything you write, decide, and learn gets stored.
- **2.7 AI as Friction Remover.** Audit your work for friction, connect AI, automate.
- **2.8 The "What's Next" Function.** Build a scoring rubric for deciding what to do next, scored by alignment with your natural advantage instead of raw efficiency. Combined score = (Advantage Score x 2) + Perishability + Irreplaceability. The unifying principle: every operator needs a system that answers "what should I do next?"

## Part 3: The Activation (Advanced)

Optimize, automate, and compound.

- **3.1 Voice-First Operations.** Transcription pipelines, voice memos as input.
- **3.2 Advanced Agent Skills.** Multi-step agents, agent-to-agent, scheduling.
- **3.3 Content as a Byproduct.** Your operating environment produces content as exhaust.
- **3.4 The Premium Path.** Done-for-you implementation as a separate offering once the foundation is in place.

## Design Notes

- **Part 0 is optional but useful.** Operators who already know they need to act can start at Part 1. The philosophical foundation is there for the ones who want it.
- **Security comes at 1.7, not earlier.** Operators learn faster when they can play first. Security is the seventh module because by then they have enough vocabulary to understand what they are protecting.
- **Part 2 is mostly templated but each operator personalizes it.** The shape is consistent. The contents are not.
