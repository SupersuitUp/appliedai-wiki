# Onboarding

*The standard implementers are held to and the curriculum operators are taken through. What good looks like, what we ship, what we do not.*

---

The Onboarding section serves two audiences, both entering the same discipline from different sides:

- **Implementers** stepping into an engagement. The standard, stated up front so the first week does not become a guessing game.
- **Operators** being onboarded into a personal AI operating environment of their own. The arc from "AI is happening to me" to "I run my work on AI infrastructure I understand."

The rest of the wiki tells you *how* to do the work. Onboarding tells you *what good looks like* when you do it, and *what state an operator should end up in* when the work is done.

## What This Section Covers

- **[Curriculum](/onboarding/curriculum).** The module sequence for taking a non-technical operator from "AI is happening" to a working personal AI operating environment. Three parts: why this moment matters, vocabulary and mental models, then implementation.
- **[Student Journey](/onboarding/student-journey).** Who the operator is at entry, the system they end up with, and the emotional arc in between. This is the bar the curriculum is held to.
- **What we ship.** The artifacts and outcomes an engagement is expected to produce.
- **What we do not ship.** The work we explicitly do not take on, even when asked, because it is downstream of strategy or upstream of execution that the operator owns.
- **Quality bar.** The standard each deliverable is held to.
- **Posture.** How an implementer behaves in the operator's environment. Confidentiality, communication cadence, escalation rules.

## For Implementers

If you are entering an engagement under this discipline, read this section first, in order. Start with [Teammate Discipline](/disciplines/teammate-discipline) for the operator hygiene principles. Then read the [Curriculum](/onboarding/curriculum) so you understand what an operator goes through when they go through onboarding. Then study the playbooks you will actually be running.

The standard is non-negotiable, but it is teachable. Read carefully. The cost of skimming this section is paid later, when an avoidable misunderstanding compounds.

## For Operators

If you are being onboarded into a personal AI operating environment, the [Curriculum](/onboarding/curriculum) and [Student Journey](/onboarding/student-journey) are written for you. They describe the system you are building, the order to build it in, and the arc you can expect to feel along the way. Read them before any setup work begins. The vocabulary you pick up here will save weeks of confusion later.
