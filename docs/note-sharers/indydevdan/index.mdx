# IndyDevDan

*A solo agentic-engineering practitioner-educator (Dan Eisler, agenticengineer.com) who argues the highest-leverage work is building the system that builds the system: composing engineers, agents, and code into repeatable AI developer workflows.*

---

## Worth tracking because

IndyDevDan ships code and content every week, and his framing tends to arrive before the industry settles on a name for it. He is early to agentic engineering as a discipline and blunt about the buzzword churn around it. The value for an operator is that he draws the actual control flow: where the human belongs, where a deterministic script belongs, and where an agent earns its overhead. He sells courses (agenticengineer.com, "tactical agentic coding") and says so plainly, so read him as a practitioner with a product, and take the framings that survive contact with your own work.

## Voice and stance

Anti-hype, opinionated, repetitive on purpose. He believes clarity of information is itself a speed advantage, so he names the primitive, strips the jargon, and beats the same drum until it lands. Where much AI commentary chases the newest model, IndyDevDan holds that the model no longer matters much: the leverage is the workflow you compose around it. He is a software engineer who does content on the side, and it shows in the bias toward separation of concerns, testing, and control flow over vibes.
