# Vervaeke and Pageau

*John Vervaeke (University of Toronto cognitive scientist) and Jonathan Pageau (Orthodox Christian symbolist and artist) on AI's civilizational effects, human meaning-making, and the case for wisdom education.*

---

## Worth tracking because

Most applied-AI commentary treats deployment, productivity, and business value. Vervaeke and Pageau operate a level down: what is AI doing to the structure of human cognition, relevance, and civilization? Their diagnostics are grounded in peer-reviewed research (Vervaeke) and deep symbolic-cultural analysis (Pageau). They name things that the applied side ignores and often cannot name.

## Voice and stance

Vervaeke: empirical, cognitive-scientific, alarmed. Cites peer-reviewed research on AI's effects on cognition, brain connectivity, and mental health. Argues from evolutionary and cognitive grounds that current LLMs are radically disconnected from the science of intelligence, rationality, and wisdom.

Pageau: Orthodox Christian symbolist. Reads AI through ancient archetypal patterns (the golem, the idol, Babel vs Jerusalem) and argues that the tool/spirit distinction is the crux. Deeply suspicious of the "AI is just a tool" framing.
