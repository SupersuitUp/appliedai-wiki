# Y Combinator

*Greenfield-only AI-native company blueprint. YC partners describe what a company built from day one around AI loops looks like. Right for net-new orgs, far-fetched for legacy dinosaurs.*

---

## Worth tracking because

YC is the cleanest articulator publishing today of what an AI-native company looks like if you start it that way from day one. Their funnel exposes them to hundreds of early greenfield orgs before anyone else sees the patterns. Their content is most useful as a blueprint for net-new orgs (their portfolio) and as a thought experiment for legacy operators (everyone else).

We do not take YC's prescriptions as universal. Their advice is downstream of a specific operating context: small teams, no legacy systems, capital-rich, talent-dense, no compliance gravity, no incumbent customer expectations. That context lets them prescribe org shapes that would be impossible to retrofit onto a multi-decade enterprise. We track YC for the greenfield blueprint and stay cautious about generalizing.

## Voice and stance

Direct and provocative. Comfortable with strong claims ("middle management is done," "burn tokens not headcount"). Operator-eats-own-cooking: YC partners build self-improving loops inside YC itself and report the results. The frame is "if you were building your company today, would you start it in this shape?"
