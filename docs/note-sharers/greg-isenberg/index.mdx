## Worth tracking because

Greg Isenberg runs a high-output YouTube channel and the LCA (Late Checkout) agency, and his format is consistently useful: bring on an operator who is actively building an AI-native business, then draw out the specific, tactical playbook in plain language. The value for an operator is the concreteness. He pushes guests past the abstraction into pricing, engagement structure, compensation, and the exact 30-day motion. Read him as a curator of practitioners rather than a practitioner-theorist: the signal is in which guests he picks and how cleanly he gets them to lay out the mechanics.

## Voice and stance

Accessible, tactical, monetization-forward. The recurring question is "how would someone actually go do this," and the recurring deliverable is a road map. He is explicit that the alpha right now is on YouTube and X rather than in universities, and he builds his content to be immediately applicable and free.
