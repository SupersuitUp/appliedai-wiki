# Three-month POC as the PE-fund cascade wedge

**Source:** Anonymous AI Consultancy founder, private 1:1 captured by Gary and Ron, ~1h29min.
**Lifted on:** 2026-05-21.

The operator runs a boutique AI consultancy with PE-fund and Fortune 100 buyers. Below is the engagement structure he walked through, lifted faithfully and anonymized.

## What I lifted

### 1. Three-month POC as the universal AI-consulting wedge → [reinforces: /playbooks/pilot-scope, → new: /playbooks/pilot-to-engagement]

Every engagement opens with a three-month proof of concept. Interview every person who touches the target function. Scope the transformation, deploy it inside a subset of the team, get them used to it. After the POC, convert into a year-long engagement and roll out across the rest of the function. The MBB firms use this exact structure to secure long contracts. It is now copyable by any boutique that can execute the structure.

### 2. PE funds as the sweet-spot ICP → [→ new: /engagement-patterns/pe-fund-cascade]

The PE function holds the budget autonomy that portcos do not. A single PE retainer cascades across many portcos through a structured request flow ("we want some AI transformation, you get these many units"). One relationship at the fund level becomes many AI transformation engagements across the portfolio.

### 3. $9K minimum, $30K+ for PE fund retainers → [reinforces: /playbooks/pricing]

Concrete pricing anchors from the consultancy's current book: $9K/month is the floor below which deals stop being worth the time, and one PE-fund retainer in the book runs ~$30K/month. Both numbers sit well under what an MBB firm charges for strategy work, which is the positioning angle: MBB-grade structure at boutique pricing.

### 4. Map the universe + improve the workflow are the two real deliverables → [field-note bullet]

Most engagements get sold as "AI transformation." The two things that actually ship and that the buyer can point at are (1) a complete mapping of the customer or account universe relevant to the function, and (2) measurable workflow lift for the people inside that function. Compresses what AI consulting actually ships into something concrete the buyer can grade.

### 5. "Supports your team, removes extra hiring needs" → [field-note bullet]

The sales-conversation disarmer for the headcount-reduction fear. Worth memorizing as a one-liner. At PE / Fortune 100 scale the framing is also honest: the orgs do not fire people because of an AI engagement, they stop adding to the function. The reframe is real, not just defensive.

### 6. Top-down via PE beats top-down via portco → [reinforces: /playbooks/finding-clients]

Going top-down through the portco CEO can stall because portco budgets are constrained. Going top-down through the PE function unlocks fund-level autonomy on spend. Add this as a channel pattern alongside trust-based and warm-intro channels.

### 7. POC structure transfers risk to the buyer → [reinforces: /playbooks/pricing]

The three-month POC is paid up front (or on a structured retainer through the window). If the buyer chooses not to convert into the year-long engagement, the operator still earns. The structure binds the buyer to a real commitment without requiring a year-long sign-on. Worth naming as an explicit pricing pattern.

### 8. The MBB POC playbook is now open-source → [field-note bullet]

The structure MBB used (three-month POC, then year-long engagement, with fund-level retainers cascading into portcos) is now copyable by any operator who can execute the shape. The moat was the playbook, not the brand. Boutique applied-AI shops can charge MBB-adjacent rates if they run the structure cleanly.

## Best quotes

> "We do our minimum amount is 9 k a month. Of course, as you get larger, we just try to go for bigger deal sizes."

> "It is always just from the PE fund itself, which is why I feel like these deal structures is the PE function has a lot of autonomy in deciding what to do. Normally, how they structure it is a bigger retainer on the fund level that they then have their portcos essentially."

> "We do a three month POC. We go out and interview every single person that touches any function in the business. Then we scope out sort of AI transformation, then we can implement the transformation for them, likely within a subset of the team for them to utilize, get used to. And then after the POC is done and the ROI that comes from it, we do a year long engagement to where we roll out to everything."

> "This sort of strategy is what the MBB firms use to secure long contracts."

> "It is meant to support your team, not to remove people. It does not really remove the person. It just removes extra hiring needs."
