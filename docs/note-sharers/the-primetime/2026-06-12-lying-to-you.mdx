# They are lying to you: the "coding is solved" evidence gap

*The PrimeTime's developer-grounded case against Anthropic's "coding is solved" narrative: 14 months of terminal flickering as the evidence, plus the broader harm of loop-everything advice delivered by people whose tokens cost nothing.*

## What we lifted

### 1. The 14-month bug gap as the core evidence
[field-note bullet only]

Claude Code shipped with a terminal flickering bug in early 2025. Known within two weeks of research release. First public acknowledgment: December 2025. The "fix" reduced flickering by 85% (a stochastic bug fix). A full rollback followed within 24 hours. The actual solution: a feature-flagged "no flicker mode" shipped April 2026, over a year after first report. This is a pure software problem, no hardware or infrastructure dependency, in the core coding tool of the company whose executive says "coding is the easy part."

If coding is solved, why does the coding tool need a feature branch to address a one-year-old rendering issue?

### 2. "Coding is solved" conflates acceleration with completion
[→ new page: [Acceleration Is Not Completion](/perspectives/acceleration-is-not-completion)]

The correct claim: AI has meaningfully accelerated code production. The false claim: coding is solved, it is the easy part. These are different claims. The second is not a stronger version of the first. It is a different claim entirely, and the evidence from Anthropic's own product contradicts it.

### 3. The loop-everything narrative causes real harm to developers
[→ new page: [The Loop-Everything Harm](/perspectives/the-loop-everything-harm)]

Practitioners are being told: stop prompting, write loops, let AI figure it out. The people delivering this advice have free and unlimited tokens. The practitioners following it restructure workflows, burn out, feel existential about their careers, and yeet code into production without controls. The harm scales with the credibility of the source.

### 4. AI labs claim capability to investors, not practitioners
[→ new page: [AI Labs Speak to Investors, Not Operators](/perspectives/ai-labs-speak-to-investors-not-operators)]

"8x the code shipped per employee" tells investors a productivity story. It tells practitioners almost nothing useful. The claim omits correctness, reliability, and the tail failure cost. Labs choose the metric that serves the investor narrative and deliver it to an audience that includes practitioners who are not the intended recipient.

### 5. "8x code shipped" metric obscures quality
[field-note bullet only]

Anthropic claims it ships 8x the code per employee versus the pre-2025 average. The metric says nothing about correctness, bug introduction rate, or rollback frequency. Shipping more code faster while also rolling back changes the day before Christmas, shipping "mysterious error messages," and running a feature-flagged flickering fix for over a year is not evidence that coding is solved. It is evidence that a productivity number was chosen carefully.

### 6. Labs have misaligned incentives on capability claims
[field-note bullet only]

The person saying "spend $10,000 a day, write loops, coding is solved" gets their tokens free and unlimited. The practitioner following the advice bears the actual cost. The incentive gap should be part of how practitioners weight the advice.

### 7. Gary's framing: this is milking humanity, and it is immoral
[→ new page: [Milking Humanity](/perspectives/milking-humanity)]

Beyond the hype cycle: AI labs are deliberately overstating capability to boost share prices. They speak to investors, not to the realities of companies, CEOs, CTOs, and individual performers who need an accurate picture to make sound decisions. The Faustian bargain: the lab cannot have internal documents acknowledging the gap, because the overclaiming must go all the way down. Employees are manipulated by the same narrative they are building. The emperor's technology has no clothes, and calling it out gets you removed from the room.
